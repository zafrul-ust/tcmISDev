Generic Bill of Lading

+ Larger Font | + Smaller Font

The Generic Bill of Lading page facilitates the printing of Bill of Ladings for materials that need be shipped from a Haas TCM hub and are not allocated to a Material Request. For example, material that needs to be shipped off site to a testing lab will need a BOL but will not appear on the Picking page. The Generic Bill of Lading page can be accessed from the Supply Chain menu by selecting Stock Room Operations. Choose Delivery. Then select Generic Bill of Lading. The Generic Bill of Lading page is launched in a new tab.

Figure 1

Select the hub where the material is located from the Hub drop-down box. Enter the ship from and ship to address in the corresponding text boxes. Look up addresses by clicking the button. Enter a description of the location in the Search Text box and click Search. Select the ship to location from the list by clicking anywhere on the line. Click OK to populate the address on the Generic Bill of Lading page.

Figure 2

You may enter the name of the person shipping the material in the Shipper ID box. You may also enter the carrier and carrier tracking number in the Carrier and Carrier_no text boxes. If the shipment relates to a Haas Purchase Order, enter the number in the PO Number text box. You may enter other notes in the PO Ref text box. Enter both the ship date and the requested delivery date. If entered on the Generic Bill of Ladings page, this information will print on the Bill of Lading.

Enter the Item ID in the Item column and the quantity in the No of Units column. Check the RQ box if this is a reportable quantity. Check the Hazardous box if this is a hazardous material. You may enter a DOT shipping name in the DOT column if you want to override the default DOT shipping information in tcmIS. Leave this field blank to accept the default DOT shipping name.

Figure 3

Click the Generate BOL button to generate a PDF version of the Bill of Lading. A sample Bill of Lading is shown in Figure 4.

Figure 4

Check the box labeled Show Inventory to print all receipts for an Item ID on the Bill of Lading. Leave the No of Units column blank. This is useful when shipping all inventory of a material to a new location, for example, if a hub is being relocated.

Figure 5

You may enter up to 25 items at a time on one Bill of Lading.
