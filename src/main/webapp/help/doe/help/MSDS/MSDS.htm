MSDS

The tcmIS MSDS Viewer page is used to locate and display Material Safety Data Sheets (MSDS). In addition to viewing the MSDS, this page can be used to view information about a material's chemical composition, physical properties, and any chemical lists on which the material appears.

The MSDS Viewer page can be accessed without logging into tcmIS. It is displayed by default when the tcmIS Home page is accessed.

The MSDS Viewer can also be accessed when logged into tcmIS by selecting the MSDS item on the main menu.

While these two versions of the page are almost identical in appearance, the differences being the main menu and the presence or absence of the ’Logged in’ notice, there are situations where you have to log in to go further than simply viewing the MSDS.

Figure 1

The MSDS Viewer page can be used to locate MSDS’s for any material that exists in tcmIS, even when the material is in another customer's catalog (although other customer information is, of course, never shown).

To locate an MSDS, use one or more ’search words’ in the Search text box to specify what you want. tcmIS will look through name data, description data, and number fields to locate the sought item(s).

There are three ’key words’ that can be used between search words to build more complex queries:

  • AND
  • OR
  • BUT NOT

These key words and the search words can be uppercase or lowercase. For example, "Florida AND oranges BUT NOT navel oranges" would return all Florida oranges that are not navel oranges.

Click the Search button to start the search.

In the first column of the data grid, the Material [ID number] serves as a link to open a child window that displays the View MSDS page.

Figure 2

On the View MSDS page

  • The product name is shown in a header area at the top of the page, as is the revision date of the displayed MSDS.
    • The header area contains two additional links.
      • A 'broken document image' () used to report a problem with an MSDS.
      • A link to the tcmIS Help pages.
  • The left column contains buttons for further information.
    • Below the buttons the NHFA and HMIS symbols provide at-a-glance safety information.
  • In the body of the page, to the right, the actual MSDS appears, either as a text document or in an Adobe Acrobat formatted version.
Figure 3

If there are any previous revisions of the MSDS, they can be viewed by choosing the revision date from the MSDS Revision Date list near the top of the left column.

Figure 4

To view chemical composition information, click the Composition button. The CAS number, constituent name, lower, upper, and average percent are displayed. The average percent is calculated using the EPA guideline displayed at the bottom of the screen.

Figure 5

To view the physical properties of the material, click the Properties button. Information displayed here may include physical state, specific gravity, flash point, and density.

Figure 6

To view the chemical lists on which a material appears, click the Lists button. tcmIS will display all the Chemical Lists and specify whether or not the current chemical, or a constituent of the material, is on each list (Yes or No). If any of the material's constituents appear on a Chemical List, the sum of the percentages of the constituents on the list is displayed in the rightmost column.

There are multiple links from this basic display of Chemical Lists.

Similarly, clicking a minus sign inside a box will collapse the list of sublists.

  • A plus sign inside a box indicates the list is comprised of sublists: click the plus sign to expand the display to show the sublists.
  • Similarly, clicking a minus sign inside a box will collapse the list of sublists.

  • Each name in the List column is a link which will open a child window to display another list: it includes all the chemicals on the clicked list.

  • In the On List column, the ’Yes’ items are also links. Click on one to open a child window which displays only those constituents which qualify the chemical for the list. For each such constituent the percentage it represents in the listed item is shown.

If there is a problem or discrepancy with any MSDS, the issue can easily be reported to the HGI Catalog Team by clicking the 'broken document image' () in the upper right of the page. Enter comments that will advise the HGI Catalog Team what action needs to be taken. Please be sure to include your name, telephone number, and e-mail address as contact information in case the HGI Catalog Team has any questions.
