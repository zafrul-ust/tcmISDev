Inventory Allocation

+ Larger Font | + Smaller Font

The Inventory Allocation process is used to allocate inventory to a material line on a Scratch Pad or Material Request. TcmIS can automatically allocate material to a line on a Scratch Pad or Material Request based on shelf life and specification requirements entered on the material line. TcmIS will allocate first to inventory held in stock in the default inventory group which meets the required shelf life and specification requirements entered on the material line. Next, tcmIS will allocate to material on a confirmed Purchase Order for the default inventory group. Next, tcmIS will allocate to open Buy Orders for the default inventory group. If no material meeting the customer's requirements is in stock, on order, or on a Buy Order, then a new Buy Order will be generated and the material line will be automatically allocated to the new Buy Order.

Note that allocations to Scratch Pads and Draft Material Requests will only last 8 hours. After 10 hours, if an MR has not been confirmed, the allocations will be dropped. Material cannot be allocated to a Quote.

To automatically allocate a specific MR line, right-click on the line and select Auto Allocate Line.

Figure 1

TcmIS displays the results of the automatic allocation. All material in inventory or in the supply chain for the default inventory group and selected Catalog Item is displayed. The allocated material is highlighted in green. Click Submit to accept the allocation.

Figure 2

You may change the allocation if needed. Enter the quantity to allocate on the desired line or lines. To unallocate a material line, enter 0 in the Alloc Qty column and click the Submit link.

Figure 3

You may automatically allocate all material lines on a Material Request by clicking the Auto Allocate link at the top of the Scratch Pad or Material Request.

Figure 4

You may forego the automated allocation process and manually allocate material by right-clicking on a material line and selecting Show Allocatable for InvGrp.

Figure 5

You may also begin the Inventory Allocation process when searching for a Catalog Item to add a line to a Scratch Pad, Quote, or Material Request. After locating the desired Catalog Item, right-click anywhere on the line and select Show Allocatable for InvGrp.

Figure 6

The quantity in inventory or on order is displayed. If the Type column displays Receipt, the material is in inventory and unallocated. The Receipt ID is displayed in the Reference column. The unit cost, manufacturer's batch number, expiration date, and remaining shelf life are also displayed. The status of the Receipt is shown in the Lot Status column.

If the Type column displays Issue-MR then a Picklist has been printed and the material is in the process of being shipped. The Quantity column displays the quantity allocated. The Reference column contains the Issue ID followed by the MR Number and line item which the receipt is currently allocated to.

If the Type column displays Receipt-MR then the Receipt ID is currently allocated to a Material Request. The Quantity column displays the quantity allocated. The Reference column contains the Receipt ID followed by the MR Number and line item which the receipt is currently allocated to. The Date of Receipt is also displayed.

If the Type column displays Receipt-SP then the Receipt ID is currently allocated to a Scratch Pad. The Quantity column displays the quantity allocated. The Reference column contains the Receipt ID followed by the Scratch Pad Number and line item which the receipt is currently allocated to. The Date of Receipt is also displayed.

If the Type column displays PO, the material is on order and unallocated. The Quantity column displays the quantity on order. The PO number is displayed in the Reference column. The expected delivery date of the Purchase Order is shown in t he Mfg Batch column. The unit cost is also displayed.

If the Type column displays PO-MR, the material is on order allocated to an MR. The Quantity column displays the quantity allocated. The Reference column contains the PO Number followed by the MR Number and line item which the PO is currently allocated to. The expected delivery date of the Purchase Order is shown in the Mfg Batch column. The unit cost is also displayed.

If the Type column displays PO-SP, the material is on order allocated to a Scratch Pad. The Quantity column displays the quantity allocated. The Reference column contains the PO Number followed by the SP Number and line item which the PO is currently allocated to. The expected delivery date of the Purchase Order is shown in the Mfg Batch column. The unit cost is also displayed.

If the Type column displays BO, the material is unallocated and on a Buy Order. This is typically material being purchased for stock. Purchasing has been notified of the demand for the material, but has not yet created a Purchase Order. The Quantity column displays the quantity on the Buy Order. The BO number is displayed in the Reference column.

If the Type column displays BO-MR, the material is on a Buy Order and allocated to an MR. The Quantity column displays the quantity allocated. The Reference column contains the BO Number followed by the MR Number and line item which the BO is currently allocated to.

Figure 7

The quantity available in the region is also displayed in the column labeled Region. Click the Region link in the top left above the data grid to view inventory in other warehouses in the region.

The quantity available globally is also displayed in the column labeled Global. Click the Global link in the top left above the data grid to view inventory all warehouses worldwide.

Figure 8

To allocate material, enter the quantity to allocate on the desired line or lines. If you are in the process of adding a line to a Scratch Pad, Quote, or Material Request, you may enter a price in the Price Override column to add the material to a Scratch Pad, or Material Request at the entered price.

Click the Submit link at the top left to proceed with the inventory allocation.

Figure 9

If the line is red, the highlighted Receipt ID does not meet the default shelf life requirements or specification requirements entered. If you are in the process of adding a line to a Scratch Pad or Material Request, you may override the default % Shelf Life Required if approved by the customer. You may still allocate to lines colored red if the customer has approved.

Figure 10

If the Catalog Item was not already added to the Scratch Pad, or Material Request, allocating material will add it as a new line. Scroll to the right to view allocated quantities in the Allocated Qty columns.

If the line has already been added to the Scratch Pad or Material Request and the customer has approved a change in the default shelf life requirement, change the % Shelf Life Requirement for the line on the Scratch Pad or Material Request to less than the remaining shelf life of the Receipt ID and view the Inventory Allocation screen again.

Note that the lines which previously did not meet the shelf life requirement but now do meet the new shelf life requirement entered are no longer highlighted red. You may enter the quantity to allocate in the Alloc Qty column for the Receipt ID and click the Submit link to allocate.

Figure 11

If you would like to allocate to a Receipt but you would like to put the MR line on hold while the stock is offered to the customer, allocate as usual and also check the box Force Spec Hold or Force Quality Hold.

This will prevent a Buy Order from being created while the customer decides if the material in inventory is acceptable. If the material is acceptable, it should be released from the MR Release page. For more information please see the MR Release help page: https://www.tcmis.com/tcmIS/help/MRRelease/MRRelease.htm. If the material is not acceptable and new material should be purchased, simply unallocate the MR Line and Auto Allocate. A Buy Order will be generated.

Figure 12

When viewing allocatable Receipts, you may view and add Receipt Specs by right-clicking on the line and selecting Receipt Specs. For more information on Receipt Specs please see the Receiving QC help page: https://www.tcmis.com/tcmIS/help/ReceivingQC/ReceivingQC2.htm#AddingSpecs.

Figure 13

When viewing allocatable Receipts, you may view and add Receipt Documents by right-clicking on the line and selecting View/Add Receipt Documents. For more information on Receipt Documents please see the Receiving QC help page: https://www.tcmis.com/tcmIS/help/ReceivingQC/ReceivingQC2.htm#UploadingDocuments.
