Create Quote

+ Larger Font | + Smaller Font

After adding material lines to the Scratch Pad, it is possible to create Quotes from the Scratch Pad. For more information on adding lines to to the Scratch Pad, see the Add Line help page: https://www.tcmis.com/tcmIS/help/OrderEntry/AddLine.htm.

To create a Quote, select the material lines on the Scratch Pad to include on the Quote by checking the box in the Grab column. Click the Create Quote link at the top left of the Scratch Pad. A new Quote is created and the Scratch Pad remains active. Click the Create Quote-Delete Scratch Pad link at the top left of the Scratch Pad to create a Quote and delete the Scratch Pad.

Figure 1

You must enter a customer PO or RFQ number in the Customer Ref field.

The expiration date of the quote is populated in the Quote Expires box. By default the quote is valid for thirty days. You may change this date by clicking in the Quote Expires box and selecting a date from the calendar.

Your name is populated in the CSR drop-down list. Choose the CSR from the CSR drop-down box if someone else should be recorded as the CSR.

You may update the Shipping Method and carrier information, if known. However, this is not required for a Quote.

Figure 2

Click the Order Internal link to add internal notes to the quote. These notes will not be printed on the Quote Confirmation.

Click the Order External link to add external notes to the quote. These notes will be printed on the Quote Confirmation. Click OK to save the notes.

Figure 3

If there are any permanent Bill To Notes or Ship To Notes, you may view them by clicking the corresponding links in the Notes area.

Figure 4

For each line on the quote, you may enter the customer line number in the corresponding column.

You must complete the Order Quantity and Unit Price if they were not already entered on the Scratch Pad.

Figure 5

For each line on the quote, select the date the customer needs the material by clicking in the Need Date box and selecting the date from the calendar. If the date the customer needs the material is the same for all lines on the Quote, enter the need date in the row header by clicking in the Need Date box in the header and selecting the date from the calendar.

For each line on the Quote, select the promised ship date of the material by clicking in the Promised Date box and selecting the date from the calendar. If the promised ship date of the material is the same for all lines on the Quote, enter the promised date in the row header by clicking in the Promised Date box in the header and selecting the date from the calendar.

The customer's default shelf life is displayed on each material line. If the customer requires a different shelf life for a specific material, you may override the customer default and enter the remaining shelf life percentage required for each line in the % Shelf Life Required column.

Check the box in the Tax Exempt column if the material line is exempt from tax. Check the Tax Exempt box in the row header if all lines on the Quote are exempt from tax.

Check the box in the Line Complete column if the material line should be shipped complete. Check the Line Complete box in the row header if all lines on the Quote should be shipped complete.

Check the box in the Lines Group column if the material lines should be shipped together. Check the Lines Group box in the row header if all lines selected on the Quote should be shipped together.

Select on from the Delivery Type drop-down list if the material line is needed exactly on the entered Need Date. Select by from the Delivery Type drop-down list if the material line is needed on or before the entered Need Date.

Select None, AOG, Line Down, or Critical from the Priority drop-down list depending on the customer's need for the material.

The default inventory group for the customer is displayed in the Inventory Group column. You may select a different inventory group if the order should be filled by another warehouse.

Enter any External Notes, Internal Notes, or Purchasing Notes associated with a material line on the Quote by clicking in the corresponding Notes columns and typing your notes.

For each line on the Quote, you may enter the customer part number, if known, in the corresponding column. Any customer Part Numbers entered will be displayed on the Quote Confirmation.

Figure 6

Customer's specification requirements should be specified when adding a line to a Quote.

From a Quote, click the in the Specification column to lookup specifications and add them as requirements for the selected material line. For more information on adding specification requirements see the Specifications help page: https://www.tcmis.com/tcmIS/help/OrderEntry/Specifications.htm.

If the line has not been confirmed, you may delete a line on the Quote. Highlight the line to be deleted by clicking anywhere on the line. Click the Delete Line link above the data grid.

Figure 7

The highlighted line is deleted from the Quote.

You may duplicate a line on a Quote. Highlight the line to be duplicated by clicking anywhere on the line. Click the Dup Line link above the data grid.

Figure 8

Additional charges like freight and packing fees can be added to the Quote header or to individual material lines. Click the button labeled Hdr Charges in the header to add additional charges to the Quote header. These charges apply to the entire order as opposed to additional line charges which apply only to specific lines on the Quote. Header additional charges can be used to create miscellaneous credits and debits witch are not associated with material lines.

Figure 9

You may add additional charges to individual material lines by right-clicking anywhere on the line and selecting Additional Charges. These charges apply to the selected material line only as opposed to header additional charges which apply the entire Quote.

Figure 10

For more information on adding additional charges to a Quote, see the Additional Charges help page: https://www.tcmis.com/tcmIS/help/OrderEntry/AdditionalCharges.htm.

You may view inventory in the warehouse and on order by right-clicking on the line and selecting Show Allocatable for InvGrp. Inventory is displayed but cannot be allocated to a Quote. Note the Unit Cost and specifications are displayed.

Figure 11

You may view inventory in the Region and Globally by clicking the corresponding links at the top left of the Inventory Group Allocation page or by right-clicking on the material line on the Quote and selecting show Allocatable for Region or show Allocatable for Global.

Right-click on any line and select Show Previous Orders for Customer to view all pervious orders for the selected Catalog Item and customer.

Right-click on any line and select Show All Previous Orders to view all pervious orders for the selected Catalog Item for any customer.

Right-click on any line and select Show PO History to view all Purchase Orders for the selected Catalog Item.

Right-click on any line and select Show Quotation History for Customer to view all historical Quotes for the selected Catalog Item and customer.

Right-click on any line and select Show All Quotation History to view all historical Quotes for the selected Catalog Item.

Right-click on any line and select Show Price List Info to view customer price list details and price breaks for the selected Catalog Item.

Right-click on any line and select Show Current Sourcing Information to view supplier price list details for the selected Catalog Item.

Right-click on any line and select Lead Time Plots to view actual lead time details for the selected Inventory Group and Catalog Item.

Right-click on any line and select Item Notes to view global notes for the selected Item ID.

Right-click on any line and select Catalog Item Notes to view global notes for the selected Item ID and Spec Group.

You may save the Quote by clicking the Save link in the top left of the header. The Quote is saved in Draft status.

To confirm a Quote click the Confirm Quote link in the top left of the header.

Figure 12

To print a quote, click the Print Confirmation link in the top left of the header. Note that only confirmed Quotes may be printed.

Figure 13

You may copy any of the lines from the existing Quote and create a new Quote by checking the box in the Grab column for each line to copy. Click the Create New Quote link in the top left of the header.

Figure 14

The selected line and header information is copied into a new Quote.

Figure 15

You can create a new Scratch Pad in the same manner. You may copy any of the lines from the existing Quote and create a new Scratch Pad by checking the box in the Grab column for each line to copy. Click the Create Scratch Pad link in the top left of the header.

Figure 16

The selected line and header information is copied into a new Scratch Pad.

Figure 17

You may delete a draft quote by clicking the Delete link in the top left of the header. The Quote is permanently deleted.
