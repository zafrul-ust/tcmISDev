Work Area Definition

The Work Area Definition page is used to view, manage, and print information about work areas set up in tcmIS. The Work Area Definition page can be accessed from the Supply Chain menu by selecting the Work Area Inventory menu item, then the Work Area Definition item. The Work Area Definition page is launched in a new tab.

Figure 1

You must choose a specific facility for the work area(s) from the Facility list. You may choose to narrow the range of work areas by selecting from the Physical Layout portion of the selection panel and/or from the Organizational Layout section.

  • Physical Layout: refers to the physical location of the Work Area.
    • You may select a specific Area from the Area list.
    • You may also further narrow the selection by selecting a specific building from the Building list.
  • Organizational Layout: refers to the work area position in the company hierarchy.
    • You may select a specific work area from the Work Area Group list.
    • You may select a specific department from the Dept. list.

Click the Search button. The work areas will be displayed in the data grid. (Because the grid contains so many columns it is illustrated in left and right sections, below.)

Click the Create Excel button to export results in Excel format.

Physical Layout columns

  • Dock is the loading dock within the facility for truck delivery.
  • The Location section contains six columns:
    Area | Building | Floor | Room | Interior/Exterior indicates whether the work area is inside or outside the building specified by the other Location columns. | (for Area, Building, Floor, and Room).
  • Compass Point is used for some facilities to help specify the work area.
  • Column is used by some facilities to identify specific spots within a building.
  • The Dock checkbox is the loading dock within the facility for truck delivery. section contains two columns:
    [Dock] Name identifies the delivery dock. and Fixed when selected indicates that there is a single delivery dock which must be used; when cleared there may be multiple delivery docks and the user selects the dock when creating the Material Request. check box.
  • The Delivery Point is the designated location within the facility for final delivery whether by HGI driver or facility personnel. section contains three columns:
    [Delivery Point] Name identifies the designated location. | Fixed checkbox when selected indicates that there is a single delivery point which must be used; when cleared there may be multiple delivery points and the user selects the delivery point when creating the Material Request. |
  • DropShip? checkbox when selected indicates that delivery is direct from the supplier and will not be routed through a HGI Hub. Labeling and quality inspection are not done by HGI Hub staff. The ship to location on the HGI PO will be the work area and not the hub.
  • Offsite? checkbox when selected the work area is not physically located at the facility. Usage is NOT included on usage reports such as Ad Hoc Usage and Ad Hoc Material Matrix.

Organizational Layout columns

  • Active? A work area may be active or inactive. If it is inactive it cannot place orders or add inventory. Status
  • Work Area [name] Every work area must have a name and must belong to a work area group. The name uniquely identifies the work area to distinguish it from all other work areas. The work area name may be edited directly in the text box in the Work Area column.
  • The Department section contains two columns:
    [Department] Name contains the department identification. A single department may (usually does) include multiple work areas. and
  • The Work Area Group associates the work area with others with which it is operationally or organizationally tied. section contains two columns:
    Group [name] a name that incorporates the Department name. The name uniquely identifies the work area GROUP to distinguish it from all other groups. and See Work Area Groups form for details.

Accounting/Purchasing columns

  • Inventory Group a list from which you may choose the appropriate inventory group for each work group. Not all organizations use inventory groups. If they are not used, the list in this column is replaced with a default text group name.
  • Account[ing] Sys[tem] a list from which you may choose the appropriate accounting system for each work group. In many instances there is only a single ‘choice’.
  • Default Charge Type a list from which you may choose the appropriate default charge type for each work group.
  • Description may be used to record a term by which the work group is commonly known.
  • Ordering Method the means by which inventory may be obtained for the work area. It is indicated and edited using the list in the column. There are three ordering methods:
    • Manual Ordering indicates the replenishment occurs when a customer places an order using the Catalog page.
    • Stock Level Ordering indicates the replenishment occurs when a work area scan indicates that additional inventory is needed.
    • Manual & Stock Level indicates that both manual and stock level ordering are used for obtaining needed inventory.
  • Days between scan is the usual number of days between cabinet replenishment scans..

ESH columns

  • The Approval Code Approval Codes are assigned to work groups as a means to control access to ordering and using Parts. Parts are assigned to one or more Approval Codes. A work area may order/use those parts assigned to approval codes the area has been given. section contains two columns:
    Approval Code[s] The list of Approval Codes which have been assigned to the work area. and
  • Daily Usage Logging? checkbox HET category.
  • Allow Separable Mixture? checkbox HET category.
  • Include Expired Material? checkbox HET category.
  • Pulled within days The number of days prior to expiration that material should be removed from the cabinet. During scanning the scanner beeps (loudly) and instructs the operator to remove the product from the cabinet this number of days before it expires.

Contact information columns

  • Contact name records the name of the contact person for the work area. may be edited directly in the text box of this column.
  • [Contact] Phone records the telephone number of the contact person for the work area. may be edited directly in the text box of this column.
  • [Contact] Email records the email address of the contact person for the work area. may be edited directly in the text box of this column.
  • Customer Work Area ID records the ID number of the work area of the contact person for the work area. (The contact person for the work area does not have to be in that work area.) may be edited directly in the text box of this column.

Action links

In addition to the Update link, there are two other links in the upper-left of the data grid:

  • The New Work Area link may be clicked to add a new work area line to the data grid. You must edit this line (as described in the sections below) to complete the information needed, and click the Update link to save the new record.
  • The Show Legend link will cause a small window to open, on top of the current page, to display a color key to any shading used to characterize the records in the data grid.

The Manage Work Area Groups page simply lists all the existing work area groups to which any particular work area may be assigned. There are three things that can be done on this page.

  • Edit one or more of the existing work area group names. The work area name may be edited directly in the text box in the Work Area Group column. No changes made on the Manage Work Area Groups page are saved until the Update link on this page is clicked.
  • Create a new work area group by clicking the New Work Area Group link at the upper-left of the page. A blank work area group line will be added at the bottom of the list: you may have to scroll down the list to see it. Enter a work area name directly into the text box in the Work Area Group column. No changes made on the Manage Work Area Groups page are saved until the Update link on this page is clicked.
  • Assign a work area group to the work area you selected on the Work Area Definition page when you clicked the magnifying glass icon . A new link, Return Work Area Group, will appear at the top of the page. Click this link. The Manage Work Area Groups page will close and the selected group will appear in the Group column. (Since you will be back on the Work Area Definition page, remember, no changes made are saved until the Update link is clicked.)
