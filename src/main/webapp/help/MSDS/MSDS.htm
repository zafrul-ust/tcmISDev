The Haas MSDS Viewer page is used by Haas TCM employees to locate and display Material Safety Data Sheets (MSDS). In addition to viewing the MSDS, this page can be used to view information about a material's chemical composition, physical properties, and any chemical lists on which the material appears. Please note that this page is not to be used by customers to view MSDS. Each customer has their own MSDS Viewer page.

The Haas MSDS Viewer page can be accessed without logging into tcmIS. It is displayed by default when the Haas Home page is accessed.

Figure 1

The Haas MSDS Viewer can also by accessed from the MSDS menu after logging into tcmIS.

Figure 2

The Haas MSDS Viewer page can be used to locate Material Safety Data Sheets for any material that exists in tcmIS, independent of which customer catalogs the material is in.

To locate an MSDS, enter a trade name, manufacturer, or manufacturer part number in the Search box. Use the key words AND, OR, BUT NOT between search words to build more complex queries. For example, "Florida AND oranges BUT NOT navel oranges" would return all Florida oranges that are not navel oranges. Sort the search results by any of the columns displayed by choosing a selection from the Sort drop-down list. Click Search.

Figure 3

Locate the desired material and click the Material ID to display the MSDS.

Figure 4

If there are any previous revisions of the MSDS, they can be viewed by choosing the revision date from the MSDS Revision Date drop-down list.

To view chemical composition information, click the Composition button. The CAS number, constituent name, lower, upper, and average percent is displayed. The average percent is calculated using the EPA guideline displayed at the bottom of the screen.

Figure 5

To view the physical properties of the material, click Properties. Information displayed here may include physical state, specific gravity, flash point, and density.

Figure 6

To view which chemical lists this material is on, click the Lists button. A list of regulatory chemical lists is displayed. If any of the material's constituents appear on the chemical list, the sum of the percentages of the constituents on the list is displayed.

Figure 7

To view a break down of the constituents on the list and their percentages, click the link in the On List column.

Figure 8

To view a summary of the list and the chemicals on the list, click the link represented by the List Name.

Figure 9

If there is a problem or discrepancy with any MSDS, the issue can be easily reported to the Catalog Group by clicking . Enter comments that will advise the Catalog Group what action needs to be taken. Please be sure to include your name and telephone number as well as your e-mail address.
