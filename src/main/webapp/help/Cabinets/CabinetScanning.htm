TCM Scan has been developed by Haas TCM to allow tcmIS to make use of barcode technology to increase the speed and accuracy of Chemical Management inventory operations. TCM Scan is a proprietary software application which runs on Portable Data Terminals (PDTs) equipped with bar code scanners. Chemical management technicians using TCM Scan can quickly and accurately inventory chemical stores while maintaining strict control over product location, lot status and expiration dates.

Scanning Tips

We think of scanning as a simple matter of aim, scan and decode. Decode is the process that occurs inside the scanner when the scanner converts the lines and spaces back into the data that they represent. Usually a few quick trial efforts are all that is required to master it quickly and intuitively. However two important considerations can optimize any scanning technique – angle and range. Some bar codes can become damaged and no longer readable by the scanner, our PDTs have a full keypad to enable manual entry when necessary.

Do not hold the terminal’s scan window directly over the barcode. Laser light reflecting directly back into the scan window from the bar code is known as specular reflection. This strong light can blind the scanner and make decoding difficult. The area where specular reflection occurs is known as the “dead zone”. You can tilt the terminal up to 55 degrees forward or back and achieve a successful decode. Practice quickly shows what tolerances to work within.

Figure 1

Any scanning device decodes well over a particular working range. This range varies according to bar code density and scanning device optics. Scanning within range brings quick and constant decodes while scanning too close or too far away prevents decodes. You need to find the right working range for the bar codes you are scanning.
The bar codes printed by tcmIS are fairly dense so it is best start scanning at a distance of about 12” from the label. If the scanner does not beep to signify a successful decode slowly move the scanner in closer.

The two dimensional bar code scanners do not employ the traditional straight red laser line but instead use an aiming pattern with corner border marks and a center crosshair.

Figure 2

When using the 2D scanner it is possible to have more than one barcode in the scan zone. When this happens the scanner decodes the symbol nearest the crosshairs.

When the scanner successfully decodes a bar code it will emit a beep and turn off the laser. You may find bar codes that no matter how close or from what angle you scan the PDT just won’t beep. This is sometime caused by an improperly formatted barcode or printer problems. The most common barcode damage in Chemical Cabinet Management is caused by exposing the barcode to strong chemicals, usually from container dripping or leakage, followed by indiscriminate wiping to clean the container carrying the barcode.
All of the tcmIS one dimensional bar codes are printed with a caption below the symbol containing the actual data encoded in the barcode. When you come across a barcode that will not decode simply use the PDT’s keypad to enter the data exactly as it appears below the barcode followed by the Enter key.

The PDTs require a minimum amount of care. However, keeping the terminal clean will avoid problems and prolong the terminal’s life. Before doing any maintenance or cleaning, power the terminal off. To clean a terminal use a clean, soft cloth dampened with a mild cleaner such as soap and water. Do not use abrasive paper, cloth or abrasive/corrosive cleaners. Wipe the entire terminal, except for the scanner window, with the damp cloth. Clean the charging contacts, keypad and scan trigger. Wipe the scanner window periodically with a lens tissue or other material suitable for cleaning optical material such as eyeglasses. Do not pour, spray or spill any liquid on any part of the terminal, particularly the scanner or scan element components.

Loading the Scanner

The PDT uses the File Xfer function to communicate with tcmIS via the host PC and the cradle. In order for the PDT to properly perform cabinet scanning functions it must download data from tcmIS. Likewise once you have completed scanning the PDT must send that data back to tcmIS via the host PC. To Download to the PDT, log into the tcmIS Java application for the customer where cabinets are located from the Java App page. From the Application menu select Cabinet Management.

Figure 8

There are two tabs on the Cabinet Management page. Select the Download to Scanner tab. The download page is arranged into several parts. On the left are list boxes for Facilities, Work Groups and Cabinets. On the right are scan list boxes for the Facilities, Work Groups and Cabinets that have been selected to scan. In the center are pairs of buttons that add or remove items from the scan list boxes.

Begin by selecting the hub which services the cabinets from the Hub drop-down list at the top of the page. Next highlight one or more facilities where you intend to scan from the Facilities list. In each list you can hold the SHIFT key to make multiple selections. Click the arrow button that points to the Scan Facilities list box. The facilities that you highlighted will move to the Scan Facilities list box. Items are removed from each list by selecting them in the appropriate scan list box and then clicking the arrow button that points to the left.

Click the Get Work Area button on the left side of the page to populate the Work Area list box. The Work Area list box will be populated with all of the work areas located in the facilities that you have chosen. From the work areas list, highlight one or more work areas where you intend to scan. Click the arrow button that points to the Scan Work Areas list box. The work areas that you had highlighted will move to the Scan Work Areas list.

Click the Get Cabinets button to populate the Cabinets list box with Cabinets located in the work areas that you have chosen. Highlight one or more Cabinets where you intend to scan. Click the arrow button that points to the Scan Cabinets list box. The cabinets that you highlighted will move to the Scan Cabinets list.

Click the Download button.

Figure 9

Note that the PDT can only scan those Cabinets that have been downloaded to the scanner. Cabinets that have been downloaded to the scanner but not scanned will not be affected in any way. Download all of the cabinets for any work area you intend on scanning. Most programs do not have a large number of cabinets so there is no harm in downloading all of the cabinets.

To begin using TCM Scan you must provide a valid tcmIS user name. You may use the keypad to type in your user name followed by the enter key or scan your user name from a bar code label. Note that if the PDT will not accept your user ID use the LETMEIN user ID to log in. When logging in as LETMEIN you will not be able to perform cabinet scans. Once you have performed the download try to login with your own user ID again. If you still can’t log in call the tech center to verify that your user ID is valid and has the necessary rights to operate the PDT.

The TCM Scan application consists of five separate programs all accessed from the Main menu. By touching the desired choice on the touch screen with either your clean finger tip or the stylus housed in the back of the PDT. Cabinet Scan is the main application which is used by Chemical Management Technicians to maintain chemical stores and upload replenishment requirements to tcmIS. File Xfer is a communications utility which allows the PDT to send and receive data to and from tcmIS via a cradle and host PC. Utilities is a collection of functions that can be used to check the status of data files on the PDT or even the PDT itself, i.e., battery level, free memory. W’house Scan, short for Warehouse Scan is used to capture an inventory count without verifying item status or location. This feature will only be used as directed by the Technical Center. Scan Test is used to test barcode labels. Logoff will return the PDT to the Log In screen.

Warning – Use only your clean finger tip or the plastic tipped stylus provided to make on-screen selections. It is very tempting to use a pen or pencil instead of the provided stylus. Pencils have very sharp points and pens have hardened steel writing points, both will permanently damage the PDT’s touch-screen.

Scanning

The Cabinet Scan application was designed for easy use but there are a few items that should be noted. Each data entry screen has an Exit button, use it to quickly return to the Main menu. Each data entry screen contains a Status window that displays the Current User, Cabinet, Bin, Count and Errors. While scanning, a record is not saved until a quantity is entered and confirmed by either pressing the enter key or scanning the next item. Pushing the Clear button will return you to the previous screen until you reach the Main menu. The Clear button cannot undo a scan that has been saved. When a bin label is scanned the time is recorded, each item scanned in that bin is marked with the same time. To preclude a duplicate count caused by inadvertently scanning a bin and its contents twice tcmIS only considers the count marked with the latest time when processing the Cabinet Scan data.

After 30 seconds of inactivity the quantity will be recorded and the display will return to the item scan prompt. When you scan your very last item press the Enter key to accept the quantity and then press the Exit button to return to the Main menu.

Scanning the cabinets will automatically generate Buy Orders or Material Requests to replenish the cabinet and notify the scanner of expired material.
