Excess Inventory

+ Larger Font | + Smaller Font

The Excess Inventory page provides information on stock which is considered to be excess. The Excess Inventory page can be accessed from the Reports menu by selecting Inventory and then Excess Inventory. The Excess Inventory page is launched in a new tab.

Figure 1

Select the Operating Entity from the corresponding drop-down list. Select the Hub and Inventory Group from the corresponding drops-down lists. You may select My Hubs or My Inventory Groups to see all hubs and/or inventory groups for which you have permission.

Click Search to see all inventory considered excess.

Figure 2

For each material, the quantity on hand, on order, and in purchasing is displayed as well as the average cost and expiration date.

Excess material in the On Hand column is defined as any OOR item in inventory as well as MM materials in inventory where the on hand quantity is above the minimum stocking level.

The On Order column shows the total quantity of the item on confirmed Purchase Orders minus any allocations of those POs to Material Requests.

The In Purchasing column displays the total quantity open on Buy Orders with no confirmed PO minus any allocation to those Buy Orders to MRs.

Keep in mind that this report only shows the excess quantity on hand, on order, and in purchasing.

You may sort by any column by clicking the column header. Click the column header again to sort in descending order.

Figure 3

If you hover above some data cells, a tool tip will pop up and display the contents of the data cell. Typically tool tips will appear on columns where the data in the cell contains over 30 characters and the default column width does not display the entire contents of the cell. Use this feature to display the contents of cells without widening the column.

Figure 4

Click the Create Excel File button to export the results into Excel format.
