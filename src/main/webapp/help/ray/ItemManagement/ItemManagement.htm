Item Management

+ Larger Font | + Smaller Font

The Item Management page is used to create buy orders for inventory groups where Haas TCM does not manage the inventory at the hub using stocking levels. The Item Management page can be accessed from Supply Chain menu by selecting Stock Room Operations. Then select Counting and Item Management. The page is launched in a new tab.

Figure 1

The Item Management page is used by Raytheon to force replenishment of stock at a Haas TCM hub, or "force a buy." MM materials with stocking levels of 0/0 should be ordered from this page. MM materials with stocking levels other than 0/0 may need to be ordered from this page if the demand for the material is more than usual and the current stocking levels are not set to accommodate this increase in demand.

To show all materials that may be ordered for a facility, select the desired warehouse from the Hub drop-down list and select the desired facility from the Plant drop-down list and click Search. All active items for the selected facility are displayed.

Check the Show Min/Max Only box to display only items stocked at the warehouse.

Figure 2

To locate a specific material on the Item Management page, enter the Raytheon part number or Haas Item ID in the Search box. Select the corresponding search criteria from the Search drop-down list. and click Search.

Figure 3

Enter the quantity needed in the Replenish Qty box. Be sure to verify the unit of measure in the Inventory UOM column and use the appropriate quantity for the specified unit of measure. For example, if the unit of measure is Tote (packaged as 275 gal tote), the Replenish Qty should be 1 and not 275 for a single tote.

Enter the date the material should be delivered in the Need Date box by clicking in the box and selecting the date from the calendar. Enter any notes to your buyer in the Notes box. Check the box labeled OK and click the Update link to process your order. The quantity in the In Purchasing column will be incremented by the Replenish Qty. A Buy Order is generated as a result of orders placed from the Item Management page.

Figure 4

Click the Create Excel button to export the data shown on the Item Management page into Excel.
