MSDS

The MSDS Viewer page is used by Raytheon employees to locate and display Material Safety Data Sheets (MSDS). In addition to viewing the MSDS, this page can be used to view information about a material's chemical composition, physical properties, and any chemical lists on which the material appears.

The MSDS Viewer page can be accessed without logging into tcmIS. It is displayed by default when the Raytheon tcmIS Home page is accessed.

The MSDS Viewercan also be accessed when logged into tcmIS by selecting the MSDS item on the main menu.

Figure 1

The MSDS Viewer page can be used to locate Material Safety Data Sheets for any material that exists in tcmIS, even when the material is in another customer's catalog (although other customer information is, of course, restricted).

To locate an MSDS, enter a trade name, manufacturer, or manufacturer part number in the Search box. Use the key words AND, OR, or BUT NOT between search words to build more complex queries. For example, "Florida AND oranges BUT NOT navel oranges" would return all Florida oranges that are not navel oranges.

Sort the search results by any of the columns displayed by choosing a selection from the Sort list. Click Search.

Figure 2

Locate the desired material and click the Material ID, which is set up as a hyperlink, to open a new window and display the MSDS.

  • The product name is shown in a box at the top of the page.
  • The left column contains buttons for further information.
  • Below the buttons the NFPA and HMIS symbols provide at-a-glance safety information.
  • In the body of the page, to the right, the actual MSDS appears, either as a text document or in an Adobe Acrobat formatted version.

If there are any previous revisions of the MSDS, they can be viewed by choosing the revision date from the MSDS Revision Date list.

Figure 3

To view chemical composition information, click the Composition button. The CAS number, constituent name, lower, upper, and average percent is displayed. The average percent is calculated using the EPA guideline displayed at the bottom of the screen.

Figure 4

To view the physical properties of the material, click the Properties button. Information displayed here may include physical state, specific gravity, flash point, and density.

Figure 5

To view which chemical lists this material is on, click the Lists button. A list of regulatory chemical lists is displayed. If any of the material's constituents appear on the chemical list, the sum of the percentages of the constituents on the list is displayed.

The display of Lists may be slower than most pages in tcmIS, taking a full 60 seconds or longer in some cases.

Figure 6

A small plus symbol (+) in a box in the first column indicates that a list itself is composed of other lists. Click on the plus symbol to display the list of lists. (The plus symbol will be changed to a minus symbol (-) which can be clicked to close the list of lists.)

Figure 7

To view a summary of the list and the chemicals on the list, click the (underlined) product name link in the List column.

Figure 8

To view a breakdown of the constituents on the list and their percentages, click the (underlined) Yes link in the On List column.

Figure 9

If there is a problem or discrepancy with any MSDS, the issue can easily be reported to the HGI Catalog Team by clicking the 'broken document image' () in the upper right of the page. Enter comments that will advise the HGI Catalog Team what action needs to be taken. Please be sure to include your name, telephone number, and e-mail address as contact information in case the HGI Catalog Team has any questions.
