US Gov Supplier Shipping

The Department of Defense requires advance notice of incoming vendor shipments. They want to know what orders are in which boxes and which pallet those boxes are on before they arrive. Scanning allows for the collection of shipment ID information and forms associations with the contents. Once all of the data is collected, the tcmIS US Gov Supplier Shipping Pages are updated and ultimately the data is sent to DoD in the form of an Advanced Shipment Notice.

Label Definitions

Unit Labels appear on each individual item being shipped. For example, if 6 cans of lubricant are being shipped in one box, each can of lubricant should be labeled with a Unit Label.

A sample Unit Label is shown below.

If the material being shipped is a drum or cylinder, and the material is not shipped in a box, Unit Labels are not necessary and External Labels are used instead.

External Labels should appear on each box (also termed Case or External Shipping Container). They may also be used in place of Unit Labels for cylinders and drums which are not shipped in boxes since the cylinder or drum is itself a shipping container (does not require to be separately packaged). A sample External Label is shown below.

An External ID appears at the bottom of the External Label. The External ID is a unique bar coded identification number assigned by tcmIS and represents a link to all of the information on the label.

An RFID Label is printed with origin and contract information. Also printed on the tag is a bar coded representation of the unique RFID encoded value. Large print identifies the labels as either RFID Case Labels or RFID Pallet Labels.

One RFID Case Label should appear on each case. A sample RFID Case Label is shown below.

One RFID Pallet Label should appear on each pallet. A sample RFID Pallet Label is shown below.

One Military Shipping Label (MSL), should appear on each case. If multiple cases (boxes, cylinders, etc.) are grouped on a pallet(s), each pallet must also have an MSL.

Supplier Shipping Home

The tcmIS Supplier Shipping Home page is: https://www.tcmis.com/tcmIS/supplier/home.do. Theses pages allow suppliers to create shipments and print the appropriate labels. Periodically there may be information that we want to pass on to our users. This information shows up under the Bulletin Menu item and is automatically selected and presented to you when there is information available (selected menu items appear as tabs).

Click Start tcmIS to log on to the Supplier Shipping Home page.

Enter your username and password provided by your Haas tcm contact. Click Login. The Supplier Shipping application is started. The menu system shows those Supplier pages that you have permission to view. Upon selecting any item in the menu system, tcmIS initiates a new tab and starts the corresponding page in the tab. Note that you may move freely between tabs and each page will “remember” the data on the page as you last left it. If you “right click” your mouse while pointing at a tab, you may add the tab to the startup procedure which will cause that tab to automatically initiate when you next login. You may notice that some tabs have been placed in your startup already.

US Gov Shipment Selection

Begin with the US Gov Shipment Selection page. This page allows suppliers to view all pending Haas tcm Purchase Orders by supplier location. Select the Supplier Parent and the Supplier Location from the corresponding drop down lists. To see all open Purchase Orders for the selected supplier and location, simply click Search.

If the Haas tcm PO number is known, it can be entered in the Search box. You may also search by Item Description, Item ID, Material Request Number, Ship-to City or State, or Ship to Zip Code.

The supplier should locate the Purchase Order lines which are to be shipped and enter the required information.

The Ship Date should be entered as the day the material will ship for the supplier's location. The Delivery Date should be entered as the estimated date the material will arrive at the destination. The manufacturer's batch number or lot number must be entered in the Lot column. The Date of Manufacture is not required, but should be entered if known. The Expiration Date must be entered. If the material is not shelf life sensitive, please enter the word "Indefinite." The Qty Shipped must be entered as well. Pay close attention to the Packaging field and be sure and enter the quantity shipped based on the unit of measure in this field. For example, if packaging is drum and the material is a 55 gal drum, be sure to enter the number of drums shipped and NOT the number of gallons shipped. It is also necessary to enter the number of Parts per Box. Again, pay close attention to the Packaging field. If the material is packaged in a drum or cylinder, and does not ship in a box, enter a quantity of 1 in the Parts Per Box field. If shipping in multiple boxes with unequal quantities in each box, click the Dupl button. This will create another line so you can enter different quantities for each box.

After completing the required information, check the OK box for each line being shipped. Then click Update Ship Info.

US Gov Labeling

After completing the Shipment Selection page, proceed to the US Gov Labeling page to print labels.

Select the Supplier and Supplier Location from the corresponding drop-down lists. You may search for all orders that need to be labeled by selecting All from the Packing ID drop-down list. Or choose the date and time when the Shipment Selection was completed. Click Search.

Check the Print box for all Purchase Orders you want to label.

Click Print Labels. Unit Labels and External Labels will be printed based on the information entered in the Shipment Selection page. The labels will be printed in sequence – namely, one external label followed by the unit labels that correspond to the quantity on the external label. Since these labels match data such as date of manufacturer and expiration date be sure to keep these together when applying labels and packing boxes.

Scanning Process

The scanning process associates External IDs to a RFID Case Label. A case may contain one or many External IDs. However, all External IDs associated with that Case RFID must be for the same order.

Cases may be shipped individually or they may be further associated with a RFID Pallet Label. A pallet may contain one or many RFID Case Labels. However, all Cases associated with the pallet must be destined for the same Ultimate DODAAC (shown on the web page).

Start tcmIS Scan on your scanner.

The Scanning Main Menu is displayed.

The features available from the Scanning Main Menu are as follows:

Scan – Packing data collection
File Xfer – Data upload to tcmIS
Clear – Delete data file
Update – Receive program and data updates from tcmIS
Exit – Return to data terminal operating system. (requires password)

Scanning - Case Association

Choose Scan from the Main Menu to start the scan application. From this screen you can begin to associate External IDs with a Case RFID.

You may also:
View Case Contents – View a list of External IDs associated with the current RFID Case Labels and remove some or all if necessary.
Add Tracking Data – You may add a Delivery Ticket Number, a Tracking Number, and Carrier name to a case record.
Pallet – Switch to Pallet Association mode.
Exit – Return to the Main Menu.

To associate External IDs to an RFID Case Label first attach the RFID Case Label to the shipping container (box, cylinder, drum, etc.) and scan the RFID label on the case to be packed.

The scanned Case RFID becomes the current Case RFID and is displayed on the screen.

Scan each of the External IDs to be associated with the current Case RFID.


To associate remaining External IDs to another Case RFID just scan the new Case RFID. The scanner will recognize the new Case RFID and update the current Case RFID. Now scan the External IDs associated with this new Case RFID. Repeat these steps until all External IDs have been associated with the appropriate Case RFID.

Scanning - Viewing Case Contents

Select View Case Contents from the Scanning Main Menu to view a list of External IDs that have been associated with the current Case RFID.

To un-associate an External ID from a Case RFID select the External ID and click the Remove button. Click the Done button to return to the Scanning Main Menu.

Scanning - Adding Tracking Data

Select Add Tracking Data from the Scanning Main Menu to enter the Delivery Ticket Number, a Tracking Number and Carrier name to the current Case RFID record.

The Delivery Ticket Number and the Tracking Number can be entered by the keyboard or scanned if available as a barcode. The Carrier is selected from a drop down list. Haas TCM will issue updates to the list of carriers periodically.

Scanning – Switching to Pallet Association Mode

There are two ways to switch from Case Association to Pallet Association mode:

The first is to click the Pallet button.

The second is to scan a RFID Pallet Label. The scanner can recognize the difference between the Case RFID Labels and Pallet RFID Labels. It will switch from Case Association to Pallet Association mode and the current Pallet RFID will be set to the scanned value.

From the Case Association screen click the Pallet button or scan a Pallet RFID Label.

From this screen you can begin to associate Case RFIDs with a Pallet RFID.

You may also:
View Pallet Contents – View a list of Case RFIDs associated with the current Pallet RFID and remove some or all if necessary.
Add Tracking Data – You may add a Delivery Ticket Number, a Tracking Number and a Carrier name to a pallet record.
Case – Switch to Case Association mode.
Exit – Return to the Main Menu.

If you switched to Pallet Association mode by scanning a Pallet RFID the current RFID has already been set, you can skip this step.

To associate Case RFIDs to a Pallet RFID first scan the RFID label on the pallet to be packed.

The scanned Pallet RFID becomes the current Pallet RFID and is displayed on the screen.

Scan each of the Case RFIDs to be associated with the current Pallet RFID.

To associate remaining Case IDs to another Pallet RFID just scan the new Pallet RFID. The scanner will recognize the new Pallet RFID and update the current Pallet RFID. Now scan the Case RFIDs associated with this new Pallet RFID. Repeat these steps until all Case RFIDs have been associated with the appropriate Pallet RFID.

Scanning - Viewing Pallet Contents

Select View Pallet Contents from the Scanning Main Menu to view a list of Case RFIDs that have been associated with the current Pallet RFID.

To un-associate a Case RFID from a Pallet RFID select the Case RFID and click the Remove button.

Click the Done button to return to the Scanning Main Menu.

Scanning - Adding Tracking Data

Select Add Tracking Data from the Scanning Main Menu to enter the delivery ticket number, a tracking number and carrier name to the current Pallet RFID record.

The Delivery Ticket Number and the Tracking Number can be entered by the keyboard or scanned if available as a barcode.

The Carrier is selected from a drop down list. Haas TCM will issue updates to the list of carriers periodically.

Scanning - Switching to Case Association Mode

To switch from Pallet Association to Case Association mode click the Case button.

File Transfer

Choose File Xfer from the Main Menu to start the File Xfer Application.

After scanning follow these directions to upload the scanned data to a PC.

1. Place scanner in cradle.

2. Start MCL-Link on the PC by clicking the MCL-Link icon.

When the MCL-Link screen appears continue to step 3.

3. Click the Press Here button to begin the file transfer.

When the file xfer is complete a success message will be displayed. The data file will be cleared to prevent sending duplicate data.

If the file xfer is unsuccessful a failure message will be displayed. The data file will not be cleared until the xfer is successful.

Press Cancel to return to the Main Menu.

Clearing the Scanner

Choose Clear from the Scanning Main Menu to access the Clear function.

Click the OK button to clear all scanned data from the terminal.

Click Cancel to return to the Main Menu without clearing the scanned data.

Updating the Scanner

Choose Update from the Scanning Main Menu to update the scanner.

1. When an update is necessary, it will arrive via email with detailed instructions. Please follow all instruction to ensure the update is installed as intended.

2. Place scanner in cradle.

3. Start MCL-Link on the PC by clicking the MCL-Link icon.

When the MCL-Link screen appears continue to step 3.

4. Click the Press Here button to begin the update.

When the update is complete a success message will be displayed.

If the update is unsuccessful a failure message will be displayed.

Pack & Confirm

The final step is to Pick and Confirm the Shipment. Case MSLs, Order Packing Lists, Pallet MSLs, and Mappings can be printed from this page.

After navigating to the Pack & Confirm tab, select the Supplier and Supplier Location from the corresponding drop-down lists and click Search. All of the label associations (Case RFID, Pallet RFID, Delivery Ticket, Tracking No and Carrier) performed during the scanning process will be shown here.

If your scanner in not operational or you did not have some of the Delivery Ticket/Tracking Information at the time of scanning, this information will be blank. It must be entered before tcmIS will allow the shipment to be confirmed and sent to Haas TCM for submittal of the government ASN.

If operating some of this page manually, enter missing Case IDs and Pallet IDs for each PO line from the Case and Pallet RFID Labels. Enter your Delivery Ticket Number. This is the supplier's proof of delivery number. If a third party carrier is transporting the material, enter the Carrier Tracking Number and select the Carrier from the drop-down list.

Check the OK box and click Update.

Once all of the data is entered and updated, the required MSLs and Packing Lists may be printed by checking the OK box and clicking, Print Case MSL, Print Order Packing List, Print Pallet MSL, or Print Mapping.

Note that there are two ways this sequence can occur.

Method 1: Here, it is assumed that the user will want to pack the pallet as they are associating the Case RFID to the Pallet RFID. However, the Case needs to be labeled with the Order RFID and Packing List which cannot be generated until the External Label/Case RFID association is uploaded. Therefore, there needs to be two upload processes, one after cases are packed and the other after the pallets are packed. The process is detailed as follows:

(A) Print and apply the External Label(s) and their corresponding Unit Label(s).

(B) Apply a Case RFID to the shipping container and associate it with the External Label(s), packing the shipping container as necessary.

(C) Upload the case information and print the Case MSLs and Packing List for each order processed. Apply Case MSLs and attach Packing List to the case(s) (shipping container(s)) for the order being shipped.

(D) If shipping pallets, pack the cases on the pallet(s) associating the Case RFID with the Pallet RFID. Once the pallet is packed, upload the scanned pallet information and print and apply the Pallet MSL.

Method 2: Here, it is assumed that all of the labeling associations can be made before the pallet is packed – either by physically associating the cases with the pallet and keeping them near the pallet but not packed or by performing the association in the office and keeping the labels segregated and ordered so that all of the packing and label application occurs at once. This process is modified as follows:

(A) Print the External Label(s) and their corresponding Unit Label(s).

(B) Select a Case RFID Label for the shipping container and associate it with the External Label(s).

(C) Select a Pallet RFID Label and associate it with the Case RFID Label. Add the Delivery Ticket and Tracking Information as available.

(D) Upload the information, print the Case MSL(s) and Packing List for each order and print the Pallet MSL(s) for the shipment. Pack cases, applying labels and packing lists according to your associations. Note it is very important that the labels are applied exactly as the associations.

After the shipment is completely packed and labeled, click Ship Confirm. Clicking Ship Confirm will generate and send an Advance Ship Notice to the government.

Examples

1. Single Cylinder Order & Shipment:

A. Select the order from the Shipment Selection Page, enter data and click the Update Ship Info.
B. Check the Print box for the order on the Labeling Page and click the Print Labels – only one External Label will print.
C. Apply the External Label and a Case RFID to the cylinder.
D. Scan the association, enter/scan Delivery Ticket/Tracking Information and upload (or simply enter the information on the Pack & Confirm page).
E. Print the Case MSL and Order Packing List and attach to the cylinder.
F. Ship Confirm.

2. Multiple Cylinders (same Order & NSN) shipped on a Pallet:

A. Select the order from the Shipment Selection Page, enter data and click the Update Ship Info.
B. Check the Print box for the order on the Labeling Page and click the Print Labels – there will be one External Label per cylinder printed (no Unit Labels).
C. Using method 2 above, collect a case RFID for each cylinder and a Pallet RFID for the shipment. Scan each External ID to its Case RFID and each Case RFID to its Pallet RFID.
D. Upload scanner and print Case MSLs (one will generate for each cylinder on the order), the Order packing List, and the Pallet MSL.
E. Apply an External Label, a Case RFID, and an MSL Label to each cylinder and attach Packing List. Pack Pallet and apply pallet MSL.
F. Ship Confirm.

3. Multiple Cylinders (same Order & NSN) shipped on multiple Pallets

A. Select the order from the Shipment Selection Page, enter data and click the Update Ship Info.
B. Check the Print box for the order on the Labeling Page and click the Print Labels – there will be one External Label per cylinder printed (no Unit Labels).
C. Using method 2 above, collect a case RFID for each cylinder and the number of Pallet RFIDs necessary for the shipment. Scan each External ID to its Case RFID and each Case RFID to its Pallet RFID.
D. Upload scanner and print Case MSLs (one will generate for each cylinder on the order), the Order packing List, and the Pallet MSLs.
E. Apply an External Label, a Case RFID, and an MSL Label to each cylinder and attach Packing List. Pack Pallets and apply pallet MSLs.
F. Ship Confirm.

4. Multiple Cylinders (multiple orders, multiple NSN, same Ultimate DODAAC) shipped on a Pallet

A. Select the orders from the Shipment Selection Page, enter data and click the Update Ship Info.
B. Check the Print box for the orders on the Labeling Page and click the Print Labels – there will be one External Label per cylinder printed (no Unit Labels).
C. Using method 2 above, collect a case RFID for each cylinder and the number of Pallet RFIDs necessary for the shipment. Scan each External ID to its Case RFID and each Case RFID to its Pallet RFID.
D. Upload scanner and print Case MSLs (one will generate for each cylinder on each order), an Order packing List for each order, and the Pallet MSLs.
E. Apply an External Label, a Case RFID, and an MSL Label to each cylinder and attach Packing List to one of the cylinders on each order. Pack Pallets and apply pallet MSLs. Note it is important to pack pallets according to the associations. You can print the mapping to remind you of your associations.
F. Ship Confirm.

5. Hardware Order shipped via FedEx

A. Select the order from the Shipment Selection Page, enter data (be sure to enter the number of that NSN you will pack in a box) and click the Update Ship Info.
B. Check the Print box for the order on the Labeling Page and click the Print Labels – there will be one External Label per box and the quantity of unit labels that will be packed in that box printed.
C. Apply an external label and a case RFID label to a box. Apply the unit labels for each NSN associated with the external label, pack those items in the box and scan the External ID to its Case RFID. Associate the Delivery Ticket. Repeat for each box to be shipped for the order.
D. Upload scanner and print Case MSLs (one will generate for each box on the order) and the Order packing List. Attach the appropriate MSL to each box and attach the packing list to one of the boxes.
E. Prepare FedEx paperwork for shipment and enter the FedEx Tracking Number on the Pack & Ship Confirm Page.
F. Ship Confirm.

6. Multiple Hardware Orders to the same Ultimate DODAAC shipped via FedEx

All non-palletized orders to be shipped individually – therefore, this scenario is the same as 5.

7. Multiple Hardware Orders to the same Ultimate DODAAC shipped on a Pallet via FedEx

A. Select the orders from the Shipment Selection Page, enter data (be sure to enter the number of that NSN you will pack in a box for each order) and click the Update Ship Info.
B. Check the Print box for the orders on the Labeling Page and click the Print Labels – there will be one External Label and the quantity of unit labels associated with that external label that will be packed in that box printed.
C. Apply an external label and a case RFID label to a box. Apply the unit labels for each NSN associated with the external label, pack those items in the box and scan the External ID to its Case RFID. Associate the Delivery Ticket. Repeat for each box to be shipped for each order.
D. Assuming the orders fit on one pallet, obtain a Pallet RFID and scan each Case RFID to the Pallet RFID.
E. Upload scanner and print Case MSLs (one will generate for each box on each order) and the Order packing Lists (one per order). Attach the appropriate MSL to each box and attach the corresponding packing list to one of the boxes on each order. Pack the pallet and attach the pallet MSL.
F. Prepare FedEx paperwork for shipment and enter the FedEx Tracking Number on the Pack & Ship Confirm Page.
G. Ship Confirm.
