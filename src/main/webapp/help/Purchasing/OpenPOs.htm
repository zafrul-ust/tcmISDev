Open POs

+ Larger Font | + Smaller Font

The Open POs page is used to enter information from suppliers regarding open tcmIS Purchase Orders. For instance, updated delivery dates and order confirmations should be entered using the Open POs page. Haas TCM employees also use the Open POs page to see what orders are open and/or overdue. The Open POs page can be accessed from the Supply Chain under Purchasing. Select Open POs. The Open POs page is launched in a new tab.

Figure 1

The Open POs page can also be accessed from the Reports menu by selecting Purchasing and then Open POs.

Figure 2

Select the hub and/or inventory group from the corresponding drop-down boxes. Select All to see purchase orders for all hubs and inventory groups. You may select multiple hubs by holding the CTRL key and clicking on the desired hubs.

Select a buyer's name from the Buyer drop-down box to see all orders confirmed by that buyer or select All Buyers the see all purchase orders regardless of the buyer.

Click Search to view all open purchase orders.

Figure 3

You may also search for all open purchase orders for a specific supplier. Click the to look up a supplier. Type the supplier name in the Search Text box, select the country from the Country text box, and click Search. Locate the supplier and click anywhere on the line to highlight the supplier. Click the Return Selected Supplier link to populate the supplier name on the Open POs page.

Figure 4

Click Search to view all open orders with the supplier for the selected hub and inventory group. Click the NONE button to clear the Supplier field.

Figure 5

Select a supply path from the Supply Path drops down list. Select Purchasing to see only orders that went through the normal purchasing process. Select Wbuy, Dbuy, or Xbuy to see buy orders that were confirmed electronically.

Figure 6

You may look up a specific purchase order by selecting Haas PO from the Search drop-down box and entering the purchase order number in the text box to the right of the Search drop-down box. Click Search.

Figure 7

You may look up purchase orders shipped via a specific freight carrier by selecting Carrier from the Search drop-down box and entering the carrier name in the text box to the right of the Search drop-down box. Be sure to change the drop-down to the right of the Search drop-down box from is to contains. Click Search.

Figure 8

You may look up all purchase orders for a specific item by selecting Item ID from the Search drop-down box and entering the Item ID in the text box to the right of the Search drop-down box. Click Search.

Figure 9

You may look up purchase orders shipped to a specific location by selecting Ship To from the Search drop-down box and entering the location name in the text box to the right of the Search drop-down box. Be sure to change the drop-down to the right of the Search drop-down box from is to contains. Click Search.

Figure 10

You may limit the results by entering dates in the Promised Ship Date From and To boxes by clicking in the boxes and selecting a date from the calendar. Click Search. Only purchase orders where the original confirmed promised date of delivery is in the selected date range will be displayed. Both the Original Promised Ship Date and the current Promised Ship Date are displayed in the results.

Figure 11

You may also limit the results by entering dates in the Date Confirmed From and To boxes by clicking in the boxes and selecting a date from the calendar. Click Search. Only purchase orders that where confirmed in the selected date range will be displayed.

Figure 12

The Expedite Age is the number of days since the last time the revised promised date/comments were updated on a PO. You may enter a number of days in the Expedite Age > _x_ days box to see all POs with an Expedite Age greater than the number of days entered. The Expedite Age is listed in the result as well as the number of Days Late. The number of Days Late is calculated based on the Original Projected Delivery Date or the Revised Projected Delivery Date. A PO is considered late if it has not been received and the current Projected Delivery Date has passed.

Figure 13

Check the box labeled Credit Hold Only and click Search to view only Purchase Orders that have been marked as being on credit hold with the supplier.

Figure 14

To update the projected delivery date on a purchase order, simply enter the date in the Revised Projected Delivery column. Click in the column and select the date from the calendar. Enter comments in the Comments column. Check the box in the OK column for all purchase orders updated. Click the Update link above the data grid to save changes.

Figure 15

If you hover above some data cells, a tool tip will pop up and display the contents of the data cell. Typically tool tips will appear on columns where the data in the cell contains over 30 characters and the default column width does not display the entire contents of the cell. Use this feature to display the contents of cells without widening the column.

Figure 16

You may mark a PO line as being on credit hold by checking the box in the Credit Hold column and clicking the Update link. Avoid adding Comments regarding credit hold as this field is often visible to the customer.

Figure 17

You may resize any column by clicking and dragging the column dividers.

Figure 17

You may sort by Promised Ship Date, Haas PO, Supplier, Item , or buyer by selecting the sort option from the Sort By drop-down list.

The data on the Open POs page can also be sorted by certain columns by clicking the column name. The results will be sorted by the selected column in ascending order. Click the column name again to sort in descending order.

Figure 17

Click Create Excel File to export the data on the Open POs page to Excel.
