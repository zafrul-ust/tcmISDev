New/Modify Supplier Request

+ Larger Font | + Smaller Font

The New/Modify Supplier Request page is used to change supplier information or enter new suppliers in tcmIS. This page is also used by new supplier approvers to review and approve or reject new supplier requests. The New/Modify Supplier Request page can be accessed from the Supply Chain under Purchasing. Select New Supplier Request. The New Supplier Request page is launched in a new tab.

Figure 1

To view current or historical new supplier requests, select a status from the corresponding drop-down box and click Search.

Figure 2

You may search current and historical new supplier requests by Supplier, Requestor, Country, or SAP Vendor Code by selecting the desired search criterion from the Search drop-down list and entering the search condition in the corresponding text box. Click Search.

Figure 3

You may limit the search of new supplier requests by request date. Click in the From and To boxes and select a date from the calendar. Click Search to see all new supplier requests submitted between the selected dates.

Figure 4

Click the New button to enter a new supplier request. Please note that you should use the Supplier Search page to determine if the supplier already exists in tcmIS prior to adding a new supplier.

Figure 5

Enter all required information related to the supplier. Required fields are denoted by an asterisk. Required information includes the supplier name, address and phone number. Optional information includes information related to the main contact person at the supplier location.

If the Remit To address is the same as the company address, select the check box labeled Same As Above to copy the address to these fields.

Figure 6

Although the Federal Tax ID is not denoted with an asterisk, it is a required field. For suppliers outside the U.S., you are not required to enter a Federal or State Tax ID, but you must enter the supplier's VAT Registration number. The State Tax ID is optional and should be completed for suppliers in the U.S. if the number is available from the supplier.

Select the default payment terms for the supplier. Please note that if payment terms below Net 45 are listed, a comment should be entered in the Notes text box. If the payment terms needed are not listed, contact the Technical Center.

Select the Operating Entity for which this supplier will be used.

Select the type of purchase. This is typically Chemical. Other options include Waste, Gas, Transportation, or Equipment.

Select the supplier's qualification level. New suppliers should be set to New.

If the supplier has issued Haas TCM an account number, enter it in the box labeled Haas Account Number.

If this supplier replaces a previous supplier due to a name change, enter the old name in the box labeled Former Supplier Name.

Enter any notes directed to new supplier approvers that should be included based on your team's specific work processes.

Figure 7

For supplier in the United States, if the supplier is a Small Business, Minority Owned, Disadvantaged, or Women Owned, or meets any of the criteria listed below the Notes text box, select the corresponding check boxes. An explanation of these statuses are below.

A small business is a business that is privately owned and operated, with a small number of employees and relatively low volume of sales. Fewer than 100 employees.

A Minority Business Enterprise (MBE) is an American term which is defined as a business which is at least 51% owned, operated and controlled on a daily basis by one or more (in combination) American citizens of the following ethnic minority classifications:
1. African American
2. Asian American (includes West Asian Americans (India etc.) and East Asian Americans( Japan Korea etc.)
3. Hispanic American - not of the Iberian peninsula.
4. Native American including Aleuts
DISADVANTAGED BUSINESS: A business which is at least 51% owned by one or more socially and economically disadvantaged individual or, in the case of any publicly owned business of which 51% of the stock is owned by such individuals; and whose management and daily business operations are controlled by one or more of such individuals. The following individuals are considered socially and economically disadvantaged: American Indians/Native Americans, Asian- Pacific/Asian-Indian Americans, Black/African Americans, Hispanic Americans.
MBE BLACK/AFRICAN AMERICAN: United States citizens and legal resident aliens whose origins are in any of the Black racial groups of Africa.
MBE HISPANIC AMERICAN: United States citizens and legal resident aliens whose origins are in Mexico, Puerto Rico, Spain, Portugal, Central or South America.

MBE ASIAN-PACIFIC AMERICAN: United States citizens and legal resident aliens whose origins are in Japan, China, Korea, Taiwan, Cambodia, Laos, Vietnam, the Philippines, Samoa, Guam, the US Trust Territories of the Pacific Islands, and the Northern Marianas Islands.
MBE NATIVE AMERICAN: United States citizens and legal resident aliens whose origins are in any of the original peoples of North America, i.e., American Indians, Eskimos, Aleuts, and native Hawaiians.

MBE ASIAN-INDIAN AMERICAN: United States citizens and legal resident aliens whose origins are in India, Pakistan, or Bangladesh.
WOMAN-OWNED BUSINESS: A business which is at least 51% owned by one or more women; and management and daily business operations are controlled by one or more women who own the business.
DISABLED VETERAN BUSINESS: A business which is owned and controlled by one or more disabled veteran. Owned and controlled means: a. A sole proprietorship owned by a disabled veteran; or a partnership or corporation of which 51% of the stock or partnership interests are owned by one or more disabled veterans; b. Management and daily business operations are controlled by one or more disabled veterans; c. A sole proprietorship, corporation, or partnership with its home office located in the United States, which is not a branch or subsidiary of a foreign corporation, firm or other foreign based business.

SBA Certified Socially and Economically Disadvantaged Businesses (SBA8(A))

A small business that is at least 51% owned by one or more U.S. citizen(s) of a socially and economically disadvantaged group can be certified as an SBA8(a) business. The 8(a) Business Development Program, administered by the Small Business Administration (SBA), helps small disadvantaged businesses compete in the American economy and access the federal procurement market. A business certified by the SBA as an 8(a) firm automatically qualifies for SDB (see below).

What is a HUB?
A HUB is defined as a business formed for the purpose of making a profit in which at least 51 percent of the business is owned, operated, and controlled by one or more:
• Black Americans - includes all persons having origins of Black racial groups of Africa;
• Hispanic Americans - includes all person of Mexican, Puerto Rican, Cuban, Central or South America, or other Spanish/Portuguese culture or origin, regardless of race;
• Asian Pacific Americans - includes persons whose origins are from Japan, China, Taiwan, Korea, Vietnam, Laos, Cambodia, the Philippines, Samoa, Guam, the U.S. Territories of the Pacific, or the Northern Marianas; and Subcontinent Asian Americans which includes persons who origins are from India, Pakistan, Bangladesh, Sri Lanka, Bhutan, or Nepal;
• Native Americans - includes persons who are American Indians, Eskimos, Aleuts, or Native Hawaiians; and
• American Women - includes women of any ethnicity, except those specified above;
who are economically disadvantaged and who have been historically underutilized because of their identification as members of these groups.

SBA- Small Business Administration

MBE- Minority Business Enterprise

Educational Institution- an institution dedicated to education
Non Profit Organization- A nonprofit organization is one that has committed legally not to distribute any net earnings (profits) to individuals with control over it such as members, officers, directors, or trustees. It may pay them for services rendered and goods provided.

Debarred- Prevented from doing business by law or regulation.

After all required information has been completed, click the Submit link at the top of the screen.

Figure 8

A new supplier request will currently be sent to two approvers before being added to tcmIS. Approvers will be sent an email asking them to review and approve the supplier. Currently, there are two approval roles for all new suppliers. The first approval role is Payment Terms, approved by Haas TCM Sourcing group. The second approval role is a general supplier approval and is performed by the Haas TCM Finance Group.

To locate all supplier requests that are pending Payment Terms approval, select Status Pending Payment Terms and click List. To locate all supplier requests that are pending general approval, select Status Pending Approval and click List.

Figure 9

To view and approve or reject a request for a new supplier, right-click on the line to approve and select Show Details. Click the Approve or Reject link at the top left of the page. You must be a designated approver to perform this action. Contact the Technical Center to add or change approvers.

Figure 10

An email will be sent to the requestor when a request is approved or rejected. If the supplier is approved, it is now possible to add the supplier to a Purchase Order.
