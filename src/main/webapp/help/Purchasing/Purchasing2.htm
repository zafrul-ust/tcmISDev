Purchase Order

+ Larger Font | - Smaller Font

Buy Orders are generated by tcmIS as the result of a material request by the client for an Order On Request or to restock the warehouse when on hand quantities for a Min/Max item fall below the minimum stocking level. The Buy Order directs the buyer to obtain material via Purchase Order.

Buyers are responsible for securing materials at competitive prices while meeting all quality requirements of the client.

Information on Purchase Orders can be found from the Supply Chain menu. Select Purchasing and choose Purchase Order to display the PO page.

Creating Purchase Orders

When creating a new Purchase Order, begin on the Buy Order page. For more information on Buy Orders, see the Buy Order help page: https://www.tcmis.com/tcmIS/help/Purchasing/BuyOrders.htm. After creating a PO from Buy Order page, the Purchase Order page is launched. If the purchase is a repeat buy, much of the required information will already be completed. However, the buyer is free to alter this information if needed.

The Purchase Order page displays a variety of information related to the Purchase Order. Information about the supplier of the material is displayed in the top left corner of the Purchase Order page. This includes the supplier name, address, telephone number, and e-mail address, if available. The Supplier ID is also shown along with the status of the supplier. In order to confirm the Purchase Order, the supplier field must be completed. Look up a supplier by clicking the Magnifying Glass. If the supplier does not exist in tcmIS, it can be added via the New/Modify Supplier Request page.

Figure 5

The ship to address and inventory group are displayed beneath the supplier information. This information is taken from the Buy Order and should not be changed by the buyer.

Figure 4

Shipping details are displayed to the right of the supplier information. The Trade Terms and Shipping Information must be completed by the buyer in order to confirm the Purchase Order. For more information on Trade Terms see http://www.iccwbo.org/incoterms/wallchart/wallchart.pdf.

Figure 5

The date the Purchase Order was confirmed is shown below the shipping details. If available, the date the Purchase Order was accepted and the Buy Order Start and End Date are displayed as well.

Figure 6

The buyer name and order taker appear on the far right of the Purchase Orders screen. The order taker should be completed by the buyer. Click the Magnifying Glass to select the Order Taker. The Order Taker's information can be edited or a new contact can be added. If there is a client PO number associated with the Haas TCM Purchase Order, then it is displayed below the order taker's contact information. Payment terms with the supplier and total dollar amount are also shown. If the PO is a Consigned Purchase Order, that is the PO will be paid to the supplier on usage and not receipt, check the Consigned PO box.

Figure 7

Clicking the Scratch Pad button allows the buyer to add notes to the Purchase Order and view existing notes. These notes are not seen by the customer or on the printed PO and are strictly for the Buyer's use. Enter a note in the box labeled Add Notes and click the Add Notes button.

Figure 8

Clicking the Email Pad button works very similar to the Scratch Pad, however, is meant to be used to keep records of emails to and from customers.

In the middle of the Purchase Order page each line that appears on the Purchase Order is displayed. The fields that are shown include: line number, Item ID, date needed, supplier promised ship date, projected delivery date to the hub, quantity ordered, packaging, unit price, extended price, quantity received, quantity vouchered to be paid, quantity returned, and PO status. Some of these fields are populated from the Buy Order. All others must be completed by the buyer.

Figure 9

Below the PO Line details, more information about the items being purchased is displayed. This includes the manufacturer's part number, the supplier's part number, DPAS rating, and client shelf life requirements. In addition, there is supplier conversion information that is used by Accounts Payable to match vendor invoices against Purchase Orders. A description of the material is also displayed in this section. The percentage of Shelf Life remaining required must be entered by the buyer.

Figure 10

The next section of the Purchase Order page describes when the item was verified by the catalog group and who verified the item.

Figure 10

The look ahead window information is displayed next. The look ahead window is often used for scheduled deliveries and OORs with delivery dates that are not in the immediate future. The look ahead window represents the number of calendar days needed to bring material into the hub and prepare it for issue to the customer. This includes supplier lead time, transit time, quality and testing time, and any safety stock time. If the number of look ahead days is known, a Buy Order will not be generated for a Material Request when it is placed. Instead, the Buy Order will be generated on the need by date minus the number of look ahead days specified for the needed item.

For more information on adding Item Notes see the Buyer Item Notes help page: https://www.tcmis.com/tcmIS/help/Purchasing/BuyerItemNotes.htm.

After all required fields have been completed on the Purchase Order, the PO must be confirmed by clicking Confirm. If any required fields are not completed, tcmIS will let you know what is missing. The PO should be printed after it is confirmed by clicking Print.

Additional Charges

To add additional charges like freight or other fees not included in the price of the material click the Add Charges button. A new line is added to the purchase order.

Figure 21

Click the to look up the additional charge item. Enter a description of the charge in the Search Text 1 and/or Search Text 2 box and click Search. Select the appropriate item by clicking anywhere on the line and click OK.

Figure 22

The selected Item ID is populated on the PO line. Enter the quantity and unit price for the additional charge. Click the additional charge line and assign the charges to the items on the purchase order associated with this charge by checking the Select box for all materials to which this charge should be applied.

Figure 23
If the invoice for the additional charge will be from a supplier that is not the same as the material, for instance a third party freight company, enter a supplier for the additional charge by clicking the to the right of the Secondary Supplier text box.

Enter the name of the supplier in the Search Text box and click Search. Select the appropriate supplier by clicking anywhere on the line and click OK.

Figure 24

The supplier ID is populated in the Secondary Supplier box.

Figure 25

Click Confirm to confirm the additional charge line.

Removing and Adding Lines

Click Remove Line and click Save to remove an unconfirmed Purchase Order line. To cancel a confirmed purchase order line, change the quantity to zero and click Confirm.

Figure 26

To add additional Buy Orders to the Purchase Order, click the Add Buy Orders button.

Figure 27

Select the Buy Orders to add to the PO by checking the box in the OK column for all Buy Orders to add to the PO. Click the OK button at the top of the page to add the selected Buy Orders to the Purchase Order.

Figure 28

To add an additional material line to the Purchase Order, click the Add Material button. A new line is added to the purchase order.

Figure 29

Click the to look up the Item ID. Enter a description of the item in the Search Text 1 and/or Search Text 2 box and click Search. Select the appropriate item by clicking anywhere on the line and click OK.

Figure 30

Highlight the new PO line by clicking the Item ID. Scroll down to the Associated PRs section of the Purchase Order and click Edit to associate a buy order from the Buy Orders page to this selected purchase order line.

Figure 31

All open buy orders for the selected Item ID are displayed. You may need to select Any Buyer to see the buy order if it is not assigned to you. You may also want to remove any text in the Ship To box to see all open buy orders. Enter the customer PO number or MR number in the corresponding text boxes to locate a specific buy order. Click Search to display all buy orders for the selected search criteria. Check the box in the OK column to associate the selected buy order with the selected po line.

Figure 32

The need date and buy order quantity are updated from the buy order. Enter the promised ship date and projected delivery date in the corresponding text boxes. Click the blue diamonds to select the date from the calendar. Enter the unit price and click Confirm.

If a Purchase Order is in progress, but not ready to confirm, click the Save button to save your progress. The buy order will remain on the Buy Orders page with status In Progress. You may return to the PO later to complete all required information and confirm the Purchase Order.

Expediting

For Open POs, expediting notes can be viewed and added for all lines on the PO by clicking the PO Expedite Notes button. Click the Enter Line Expediting Notes button to view and add expedite notes for only the selected PO line.
