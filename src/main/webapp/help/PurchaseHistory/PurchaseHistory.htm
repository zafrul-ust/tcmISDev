Purchase History

+ Larger Font | + Smaller Font

The Purchase History page is used to lookup and view historical Purchase Orders in tcmIS. This page can be accessed from the Supply Chain Menu. Under Purchasing, select Purchase History. The Purchase History page is launched in a new tab.

Figure 1

You may also access the Purchase History page from the Reports menu by selecting Purchasing then Purchase History.

Figure 2

To locate the desired Purchase Order, first select the Hub and/or Inventory group, if known. If not known, select All. You may select multiple hubs by clicking and holding the CTRL key.

Select the buyer's name, if known. If the buyer is not known, select Any Buyer.

You may limit the Purchase Orders displayed by Promised Date. Enter a date range in the Original Promised Date From and To boxes by clicking in the corresponding boxes and selecting a date from the calendar.

You may also limit the Purchase Orders displayed by Date Confirmed. Enter a date range in the Date Confirmed From and To boxes by clicking in the corresponding boxes and selecting a date from the calendar.

Click Search to display all Purchase Orders which meet the search criteria.

Figure 3

You may also search by Item Description, Item Id, Customer PO Number, Part Number or Supplier Name. Select the desired search criteria from the Search drop-down box. Enter the corresponding data in the the text box labeled for.

Figure 4

It is possible to view the entire purchase history of a material or supplier by setting the search criteria on the left to All and enter an Item ID, Item Description, or supplier.

Figure 5

To export results into Excel, click Create Excel.

The data on the Purchase History page can be sorted by certain columns by clicking the column name. The results will be sorted by the selected column in ascending order. Click the column name again to sort in descending order.

Figure 6

Click the icon in the top right of the Search POs page to collapse the search header. Click the icon to display the search header.

You may resize any column by clicking and dragging the column dividers.

Figure 7

If you hover above some data cells, a tool tip will pop up and display the contents of the data cell. Typically tool tips will appear on columns where the data in the cell contains over 30 characters and the default column width does not display the entire contents of the cell. Use this feature to display the contents of cells without widening the column.

Figure 8

To view the Purchase Order, right-click on the line and select View Purchase Order. The PO is launched in a new tab. Alternately, you may click the PO number in the PO column to view the Purchase Order as well.
