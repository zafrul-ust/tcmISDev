Scheduled Deliveries: Advantages and Cautions

Scheduled Deliveries are useful in many situations, but are best used for items with predictable usage or for items with long lead times and/or short shelf life. Due to the complex nature of scheduled deliveries, it is suggested that you discuss the requirement with your supervisor, site inventory planners, and your HGI contact prior to use. Please review these advantages and cautions to assist you and your management team.

Advantages of Scheduled Deliveries

  • Provide a reliable and timely stream of material to the work area.
    • HGI buyers work with manufacturers to schedule runs to meet delivery expectations.
    • Alleviate concerns about overlooking quantity check-based reordering, especially for materials with short shelf life.
    • Users only need to plan for the lead time of the initial shipment. HGI buyers are responsible for calculating all other lead times in order to meet the remaining schedule.
  • Insure proper allocation of material to the work area.
    • This dedicated inventory is not normally available to other work areas or and is not ever sent to other customers.
  • Insure that one-time buys of material are not incorporated into Min/Max set points (typically based on site usage patterns). This option can be used even if only one delivery is required.
  • Reduce the amount of inventory stored at the HGI Hub under Min/Max guidelines (typically stock 90 days usage) thus reducing potential exposure to obsolete, scrap and expired product.
    • Purchasing material as an OOR also reduces exposure risk, but it requires end users to calculate lead times into their planning for every shipment.

Cautions concerning Scheduled Deliveries

  • Typically based on current usage; if usage levels change, the schedule needs to be updated.
    • If the schedule is not updated, excess or shortage of material might occur.
    • If/when a Scheduled Delivery expires, no additional material will be stocked at the HGI Hub until the requirement is renewed or extended.
  • Changes need to incorporate normal lead times for material when the change is for an upward ramp.
    • Schedules do not incorporate any "safety stock". For an increase normal OOR lead times might apply (3-6 weeks in many instances).
    • Orders placed under normal MM inventory typically maintain 90 days of historical usage and thus a fair amount of safety stock is typically available.
  • These inventories are a commitment for future usage.
    • Cancellation or changes to the order may be restricted when (1) confirming orders are placed with manufactures, (2) materials are in route, or (3) materials are at the HGI Hub or customer site.
    • Any material HGI is unable to cancel or negotiate to return is the responsibility of the requestor. This can result in a significant amount of excess, dead, and scrap inventory if not managed cautiously.
  • Scheduled Deliveries are not included in Min/Max calculations for inventory stored at the Hub.
  • Because material is allocated to the specific work area, others work areas are not authorized to draw from this stock unless specific instructions are given to the Hub.

On the Delivery Schedule page repeated deliveries can be scheduled for specified dates or on a regularly scheduled (recurrent) basis.

  • To schedule a delivery for specific dates:
    • click in the text box in the Scheduled column. When the date selection form appears choose the needed date(s). Enter the quantity to be delivered on each date in the Current Quantity column. Click the Update link to save this delivery.
    • For more lines to add delivery dates, right-click on a row, select the Add row menu item, and provide the needed information. Repeat the process to add as many deliveries as needed.
    • To remove a delivery date right-click on the delivery date line and select the Remove menu item.

    • The Frequency page will open in a new window.

  • ...schedule recurring deliveries (continued)
    • Enter the quantity for each delivery in the Quantity text box.
    • Indicate the frequency using a combination of the Frequency options (Monthly, Weekly, or Daily) and their corresponding quantity boxes (Every [fill in the blank] Months/Weeks/Business Days). For example
      • If one item should be delivered per month, enter the number 1 in the Quantity box, select Monthly, and enter 1 in the box labeled Every x Months box.
      • If nine items should be delivered every 2 weeks, enter the number 9 in the Quantity box, select Weekly, and enter 2 in the box labeled Every x Weeks box.
      • If five items should be delivered every 3 (business) days, enter the number 5 in the Quantity box and enter 3 in the Every x Business Days box.
    • Enter the total quantify for the entire schedule in the Total to Deliver box. (The total quantity divided by the quantity per delivery will determine how many actual deliveries there will be, and therefore the 'lifetime' of the schedule.)
    • Specify the date on which the first scheduled delivery is to occur in the Starting Date box. Click in the box and select the date on the Scheduled Date Selector form.
    • Finally, click the OK button to save the schedule for the line item.

If the total quantity of the order was changed in the delivery schedule process, a dialog box will appear notifying you that the scheduled quantity is different than the ordered quantity. Clicking the Yes button updates the quantity in the original order and displays the Delivery Schedule page. Clicking the Cancel button redisplays the Delivery Frequency page so you can adjust the schedule.

The Delivery Schedule page Calendar allows you to browse through the delivery schedule one month at a time, moving forward or backward with the ’double chevron’ (<< or >>) links in the upper-left and upper-right corners.

  • Edit the quantity of any scheduled delivery by clicking on a day and entering a new quantity.
  • Remove a delivery by entering a quantity of zero.
  • Add a delivery by clicking on a date and entering the quantity to be delivered.
  • Click the Update link at the upper left to save changes.

Click the Show Summary link to return to the Delivery schedule screen.

After creating or changing a schedule on the Delivery Schedule page, there are two possible ways to finish. It is critically important that you first click the Update link to preserve the changes you have made.

  • If there is a Return to MR link on the Delivery Schedule page, click it to close the page and return to the updated Material Request page.
  • If there is not a Return to MR link, simply close the Delivery Schedule page by closing the child window.
