New Supplier Request

The New Supplier Request page is used to initiate new supplier records. The New Supplier Request page is accessed by clicking on the New link on the New/Modify Supplier Requests page. The New Supplier Request page is launched in a new tab.

The New Supplier Request may also be accessed by clicking on the New Supplier button on the Supplier Search page.

Use the Supplier Search page to determine if the supplier already exists in tcmIS before requesting a new supplier record.

A new supplier record is not completed until all required information is provided and it has been reviewed and approved by new supplier approvers.

The New Supplier Request form is quite long. In the figures below is has been broken into sections for easier reference.

Required fields are denoted by an asterisk and information must be supplied for each of them. It is advisable to provide other information insofar as possible.

The top of the form provides three action links and two address sections. All the information in the address sections is required.

  • The Save link is used to preserve a newly created request before it is ready for submission and approval. Once a request is Saved it will be stored with a Draft status until it is completed and submitted.
  • The Submit link is used after the form is complete to submit the request for review by the new supplier approvers.
  • The Delete link is used to discard a request that has not been submitted.

Both address sections include the same required information (except the supplier name information is not repeated.) Begin with the Country drop-down list so that the State/Province drop-down list has the appropriate items.

If all the information in the Submit To section is the same as that provided in the Address section, simply select the Same As Above check box to copy the information. Otherwise complete all the required fields.

Figure 2

The next two sections call for contact and business information.

  • Although the Federal Tax ID is not denoted with an asterisk, it is a required field. For suppliers outside the U.S., use this field to enter the VAT number.
    • In some cases the Federal Tax ID is the supplier's personal Social Security Number. In this case, select the check box to the left of SSN.
  • The State Tax ID is optional but should be completed for suppliers in the U.S. if the number is available from the supplier.
  • Select the Type of Purchase from the drop-down list. This is typically Chemical. Other options include Waste, Gas, Computer Hardware, and Other.
  • Select the supplier's Qualification Level from the drop-down list. New suppliers should be set to New. If the customer has directed HGI to purchase from this supplier select Customer Directed-New.
Figure 3

The next two sections are small tables used to provided payment terms and payment terms exceptions. Both tables originally have no information. To add information to either table, as appropriate, click the Add Line link at the top of the table. tcmIS will insert a new line in the table where you may:

  • Choose the Operating Entity for which this supplier will be used from the drop-down list that appears when you click into the text box.
  • For Exceptions only select the Inventory Group to which the exception applies from the drop-down list that appears when you click into the text box.
  • Select the Payment Terms the supplier has extended to the selecting Operating Entity from the drop-down list that appears when you click into the text box.
    • If the payment terms needed are not listed, contact the Technical Center.

To remove a row click anywhere on the line and click the Remove Line link.

The Misc[ellaneous] section has several functions.

  • If this supplier replaces a previous supplier due to a name change, enter the old name in the Former Supplier Name text box.
  • Enter any notes directed to new supplier approvers that should be included based on your team's specific work processes.
  • Use the set of check boxes to indicate which of the various classifications apply to the supplier. (Mouse over any classification for more information.)
    • Small Business A Small Business is one that is privately owned and operated, with a small number of employees and relatively low volume of sales. Fewer than 100 employees. (rule of thumb) See also the U.S. Small Business Administration for details.
    • Minority Business An American term: a business which is at least 51% owned, operated, and controlled on a daily basis by one or more (in combination) American citizens of the following ethnic minority classifications:
      1. African-American
      2. Asian-American (includes West Asian Americans (India etc.) and East Asian Americans( Japan Korea etc.)
      3. Hispanic-American - not of the Iberian peninsula
      4. Native American including Aleuts
    • Disadvantaged Business At least 51% owned by one or more socially and economically disadvantaged individual or, in the case of any publicly owned business of which 51% of the stock is owned by such individuals; and whose management and daily business operations are controlled by one or more of such individuals. The following individuals are considered socially and economically disadvantaged: American Indians/Native Americans, Asian Pacific/Indian-Americans, Black/African-Americans, Hispanic-Americans.
    • ISO 9001 Certified The International Standard for Quality Management Systems (QMS). It provides a set of principles that ensure a common sense approach to the management of business activities to consistently achieve customer satisfaction.
    • AS 9120 Certified This is the Standard requirements for a Quality Management System based on AS 9100 which adds 100+ additional requirements that are specific to distributors who carry aircraft components like fasteners, electronics, gaskets, etc. It helps ensure that they handle the materials properly and track the part from OEM to customer. AS9120 was developed for pass-through distributors of aerospace items and addresses chain of custody, traceability, control and availability of records.

    • MBE with Local Certified TBD
    • WBE with Local Certified TBD
    • SDB with SBA Certified TBD
    • Category 8A With SBA Certified At least 51% owned by one or more U.S. citizen(s) of a socially and economically disadvantaged group that is certified as an SBA8(a) business. The 8(a) Business Development Program, administered by the Small Business Administration (SBA). A business certified by the SBA as an 8(a) firm automatically qualifies for SDB (see below).
    • Hub With SBA Certified A business formed for the purpose of making a profit in which at least 51 percent of the business is owned, operated, and controlled by one or more who are economically disadvantaged and who have been historically underutilized because of their identification as members of these groups:
      1. Black Americans: includes all persons having origins of Black racial groups of Africa;
      2. Hispanic-Americans: includes all person of Mexican, Puerto Rican, Cuban, Central or South America, or other Spanish/Portuguese culture or origin, regardless of race;
      3. Asian-/Pacific-Americans: includes persons whose origins are from Japan, China, Taiwan, Korea, Vietnam, Laos, Cambodia, the Philippines, Samoa, Guam, the U.S. Territories of the Pacific, or the Northern Marianas; and Subcontinent Asian-Americans which includes persons who origins are from India, Pakistan, Bangladesh, Sri Lanka, Bhutan, or Nepal;
      4. Native Americans: includes persons who are American Indians, Eskimos, Aleuts, or Native Hawaiians; and
      5. American Women: includes women of any ethnicity, except those specified above.
    • Educational Institution An institution dedicated to education
    • MBE Black American Operated by United States citizens and legal resident aliens whose origins are in any of the Black racial groups of Africa.
    • MBE Hispanic American Operated by United States citizens and legal resident aliens whose origins are in Mexico, Puerto Rico, Spain, Portugal, Central or South America.
    • MBE Asian Pacific American Operated by United States citizens and legal resident aliens whose origins are in Japan, China, Korea, Taiwan, Cambodia, Laos, Vietnam, the Philippines, Samoa, Guam, the US Trust Territories of the Pacific Islands, and the Northern Marianas Islands.
    • AS 9100 Certified The quality management system (QMS) standard specific to the aerospace industry.
    • MBE Native American Operated by United States citizens and legal resident aliens whose origins are in any of the original peoples of North America, i.e., American Indians, Eskimos, Aleuts, and native Hawaiians.
    • MBE Asian Indian American Operated by United States citizens and legal resident aliens whose origins are in India, Pakistan, or Bangladesh.

    • Woman Owned At least 51% owned by one or more women; and management and daily business operations are controlled by one or more women who own the business.

      1. A sole proprietorship owned by a disabled veteran; or a partnership or corporation of which 51% of the stock or partnership interests are owned by one or more disabled veterans;
      2. Management and daily business operations are controlled by one or more disabled veterans;
      3. A sole proprietorship, corporation, or partnership with its home office located in the United States, which is not a branch or subsidiary of a foreign corporation, firm or other foreign based business.
    • Non Profit Organization A nonprofit organization is one that has committed legally not to distribute any net earnings (profits) to individuals with control over it such as members, officers, directors, or trustees. It may pay them for services rendered and goods provided.
    • DEBARRED Prevented from doing business by law or regulation.

After all required information has been completed, click the Submit link at the top of the page. A new supplier request notification will be e-mailed to two approvers asking them to review the supplier request. See Review/Approve/Reject Supplier Request" for details.
