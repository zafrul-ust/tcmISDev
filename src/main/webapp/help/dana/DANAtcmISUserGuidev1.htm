Installation

TcmIS requires that you make Internet Explorer you default browser before installation:

  • From your desktop, click the Start button, and choose Programs. Select Internet Explorer from the Programs menu.
  • From the Tools menu, choose Internet Options.
  • Select the Programs tab at top of the screen.
  • At the bottom of the Programs tab, click the check box labeled Internet Explorer should check to see whether it is the default. Click Apply, and then click OK. (If Apply is inactive, just click OK.)
  • Close Internet Explorer and any other programs that are running.
  • Restart Internet Explorer. A message will appear asking if you want to make Internet Explorer your default browser. Click Yes. Also select Do Not Ask Again. (If you do not receive this message then Internet Explorer was already established as your default browser.)

You are now ready to begin the installation of tcmIS:

  • Open Internet Explorer and enter the URL for your company: (If typing the address, type as shown, with capital letters in address capitalized.)
    • Dana: https://www.tcmis.com/tcmIS/dana/Register
  • When the page opens:
    • click . If you receive a Security Alert message, select In the Future Do Not show this warning.
    • From the File Download window, click Open.
    • Accept the license agreement by clicking Yes.
    • Select Typical when prompted to choose a setup type. Then click Next.
  • Using Internet Explorer, return to the URL listed above for your company.
    • Add this page to your Favorites. In Internet Explorer, from the Favorites menu, choose Add to Favorites.
    • Enter your username and password.
    • Java Web Start will check for the latest version. Once complete you will see a Java Web Start Security Warning: "This application is requesting unrestricted access to your local machine and network". Click Start.
  • Installation is complete and tcmIS will be launched.
  • To launch the new version of tcmIS in the future, open Internet Explorer and select tcmIS from your list of Favorites.

In addition to viewing the MSDS, this page can be used to view information about a material's chemical composition, physical properties, and any chemical lists on which the material appears. The MSDS Viewer page can be found at: https://www.tcmis.com/tcmIS/dana/Msds.

Chemical and Gas Catalog

The Chemical and Gas Catalog contains information on materials approved for use in the specified facility and work area. There are currently two types of catalogs. The Part Catalog identifies all materials and their requirements that have been defined for the specified facility and/or work area as well as any ordering restrictions. The Item Catalog is more general and represents all chemicals defined by manufacturers that have been ordered by any Haas TCM customer.

Part Catalog

All materials that have been ordered or are allowed to be ordered are stored in the Part Catalog. Information about materials can be entered in the Search box. Search criteria can include customer part number, Haas TCM part number, packaging, trade name, manufacturer, manufacturer's part number, or material description. The more specific the information entered, the more the search is narrowed. Multiple words as a logical expression can also be used to refine a search (separate words by AND, OR, NOT; for example, acetone AND cleanroom). To search for all chemicals for a specific location, leave the Search box blank and click the Search button.

Some additional constraints can be added to a search of the Part Catalog. Selecting the Active for selected Facility/Work Area option displays only those chemicals associated with the selected facility and work area. Selecting the Approved Only check box limits the search to only those chemicals approved for the selected facility and work area.

Selecting the All Catalogs option displays chemicals for all catalogs associated with your company. This feature can be useful when searching for a new material. Selecting the Active Only check box limits the search to chemicals that are currently active. A chemical is inactive if it becomes obsolete for the part number, is no longer manufactured, or is not yet approved for the part number.

The Part Catalog is divided into three sections. The columns in blue and white represent the part details and include catalog, customer part number, part description, stocking type (MM or OOR), shelf life, and price. The first two columns in green and white represent the item details pertaining to the part. The rest of the columns give component level details such as packaging, manufacturer, and approval status.

Chemical or Gas Order Placement

The next step in ordering a chemical is to add it to the shopping cart. After locating a chemical in the Part Catalog, select it by double-clicking on the line in the part detail area, or clicking once and then clicking the Add button in the shopping cart. Searching and adding chemicals to the shopping cart can be repeated until all needed chemicals are in the shopping cart. Once a request has been added to the shopping cart, all further additions to that cart must be for delivery to the same facility.

Figure 21

If a material with a comment attached to it is added to the shopping cart, the comment will be displayed. After viewing the comment, clicking Continue will add the part to the shopping cart. Clicking Cancel will continue without adding the part to the shopping cart.

After adding chemicals to the shopping cart, the quantity can be changed by double clicking on the current Part Quantity and entering the quantity needed. The quantity can also be changed on the Material Request screen which is described in depth below.

An item can be removed from the shopping cart by clicking on the corresponding line and clicking the Delete Line button.

Once all needed chemicals have been added to the shopping cart, the next step in completing the order is to check out. When the order is ready to submit, clicking on the Check Out button will display the Material Request screen shown below.

Figure 22

The Material Request screen allows the chemical requestor to assign the delivery point, need by date, scheduled delivery dates, and charge numbers. This must be done for each line item on the request. The quantity ordered may also be adjusted on this screen.

The requestor may enter the End User Name and Department in the corresponding text boxes to indicate the point-of-use for the Material Request. This information applies to all line items on the request. The information from the End User and Department boxes will appear on the delivery notice. These fields are flexible, and remarks can be customized to assist the end user once the item is delivered.

The requestor may also complete the Notes and Critical fields. The Notes field provides a dialog box for entering information that needs to be communicated to the Haas TCM purchasing team. Selecting the Critical box notifies the Haas TCM purchasing team that this is a Priority 1 buy. A Priority 1 buy is an order in which a late delivery will shut down production or the item's lead-time is long enough to create a problem with operations.

If the requestor works for a facility that uses the Financial Approval functionality and the Material Request exceeds the amount the requestor is approved to order, further approval is required. A list of people who can approve the order can be viewed by clicking on the pending link in the header section of the Material Request screen as shown in Figure 22. When the Material Request is submitted, it will be routed to the appropriate approver.

Figure 23

Delivery Scheduling

The next step in completing the Material Request screen is to specify the date the material is needed. Note that the process of assigning a need date must be completed for each line item on the Material Request screen.

There are two types of need dates: those that are needed on or before a specified date, and those that are needed exactly on a specified date or set of dates. The latter deliveries are referred to as Scheduled Deliveries.

Select Deliver By or Schedule from the Delivery Instructions drop-down list. If an on or before date is needed, choose Deliver By and specify the requested deliver by date. This selection will inform Haas TCM to deliver the material on or before the specified date.

Figure 24

If a delivery needs to be scheduled for a specific date, whether it is a single delivery or multiple deliveries, select Schedule from the Delivery Instructions list and click View Schedule. The Delivery Schedule screen will be displayed.

In the Delivery Schedule screen, deliveries can be scheduled on a regular basis or on specified dates. A calendar is provided to select the delivery dates. To schedule a delivery on a specific date, click the calendar date and enter the quantity to be delivered in the Quantity to deliver on: box, and press ENTER. The quantity will then appear on the calendar on the date it is to be delivered as shown in Figure 25.

Figure 25

Continue this process for all desired delivery dates. The Previous and Next buttons move between calendar months. Clicking Summary displays a summary of all the scheduled delivery dates. In the Delivery Schedule Summary screen, deliveries can be deleted or added by clicking on a delivery, highlighting it. Click Delete to delete the selected delivery. Click New to add another delivery. Clicking the Go To button displays the Delivery Schedule screen. When all deliveries have been scheduled, click OK.

Figure 26

If deliveries should be scheduled on a set frequency, click Frequency.

Figure 27

In the Quantity box, enter the number of items to be delivered for each of the scheduled deliveries. For example, if one item should be delivered per month, enter the number 1 in the Quantity box and click Monthly. The date of the first delivery should be entered in the Starting Date box. If deliveries need to be scheduled on a more frequent basis than monthly, the Daily option should be selected. For the daily scheduling option, each day represents a calendar day. If a scheduled delivery is requested on a daily frequency and one of those days falls on a weekend, Haas TCM will deliver the item on the Friday preceding the weekend.

If the total quantity of the order was changed in the delivery schedule process, a dialog box will appear notifying the requestor that the scheduled quantity is different than the ordered quantity. Clicking Yes updates the quantity in the original order. Clicking No displays the Delivery Schedule screen so the requestor can adjust the schedule.

Figure 28

Scheduled Deliveries are useful in many situations, but they are best used for items with consistent usage or for items with long lead times and/or short shelf life. Due to the complex nature of scheduled deliveries, it is suggested that you discuss the requirement with your supervisor, site inventory planners, and your Haas TCM contact prior to use. Please review some of the examples of advantages and cautions below to assist you and your management team.

Advantages

  • Scheduled Deliveries act as a forecast which allows Haas TCM buyers to provide manufacturers ample time to schedule runs to meet end user demands.
  • Scheduled Deliveries provide an easy, hands free method for a steady stream of products to arrive at the end user location.
  • Scheduled Deliveries insure allocation of material to a specific work area, not allowing other work areas or customers to pull from inventory planned and set aside for the Scheduled Delivery.
  • Scheduled Deliveries help insure a steady stream of product to a work area. If a material has a short shelf life, the work area might not order replenishments in a timely manner (prior to product expiring) if they replenish by doing a quantity check. Scheduled Deliveries will help insure new product is constantly arriving.
  • Scheduled Deliveries can be used to insure one-time buys of material are not incorporated into Min/Max set points (typically based on site usage patterns). This option can be used even if only one delivery is required.
  • Scheduled Deliveries can reduce the amount of inventory stored at the Haas TCM Hub under Min/Max guidelines (typically stock 90 days usage) thus reducing potential exposure to obsolete, scrap and expired product. Purchasing material as an OOR also reduces exposure risk, but requires end users to calculate lead times into their planning for every shipment. When orders are placed via Scheduled Deliveries, end users only need to plan for the lead time of initial shipment. Haas buyers are responsible for calculating all other lead times in order to meet the remaining schedule.

    Cautions

  • Scheduled Deliveries are typically based on current usage. Should usage levels change, the Scheduled Delivery also needs to be updated. If the Scheduled Delivery is not updated, excess material could be delivered or a shortage of material might occur.
  • Changes in Scheduled Deliveries need to incorporate normal lead times for material when the change is for an upward ramp.
  • Materials ordered as Scheduled Deliveries do not incorporate any "safety stock". Should the end user require more inventory in a given time period, normal OOR lead times might apply (3-6 weeks in many instances). Orders placed under normal MM inventory typically maintain 90 days of historical usage and thus a fair amount of safety stock is typically available.
  • Scheduled Deliveries are a commitment for future usage. Should confirming orders be placed with manufactures or replenishment inventory be in route or at the Haas TCM Hub or customer site, cancellation or changes to the order could have restrictions. Any quantities Haas TCM is unable to cancel or negotiate returns will be the responsibility of the requestor. Scheduled Deliveries could result in a significant amount of excess, dead and scrap inventory on site if not managed cautiously .
  • Scheduled Deliveries are not included in Min/Max calculations for inventory stored at the Hub. Should your Scheduled Delivery expire, no additional material will be planned to be stocked at the Hub unless the requirement is clearly communicated.
  • Because material ordered as Scheduled Deliveries are allocated to the specific work area, others work areas are not authorized to draw from this stock unless specific instructions are given to the Hub.

Some facilities have multiple accounting systems, and the requestor will be prompted to specify which accounting system to use immediately after clicking Check Out. If there are multiple accounting systems, one must be selected from the drop-down box labeled Select an Account System. The selected accounting system must be used for all items in the shopping cart.

Figure 29

For the % column, enter the percentage of the line item to be charged to that number. The percentages for each line item must total 100%.

Figure 30

Order Submission

The final step in placing a chemical or gas order is to submit the order. Before submitting the order, the requestor should review the order for accuracy and completeness. If the requestor does not have all of the required information, clicking Save will save the Material Request at draft status. Draft orders can be retrieved and completed through the Order Tracking screen. Individual lines can also be removed from a Material Request by selecting the line and clicking the Delete Line button. The entire Material Request can be deleted by clicking the Delete button. If more parts need to be added to the order, the Return to Cart button can be used to return to the Chemical Catalog.

Check Scrap/Obsolete if this order should be filled from material in inventory that has been declared scrap or obsolete.

Click Submit to complete the process of placing the order. If the order is for a part that is an OOR whose item is currently in stock, tcmIS will prompt the requestor to allow the OOR shelf life requirement to we waived to allow the stocked item to be shipped immediately. The requestor is informed of the minimum remaining shelf life for the delivered items from stock. Often this material is currently owned by the requestor's company and may be a lost value if no one accepts the material. If this transaction is authorized, tcmIS will attempt to fill the request immediately. There are some occasions where shipping an OOR from stock could threaten the ability to ship the stocked item to the Min/Max requirement. In these cases, the order will revert to normal OOR processing.

The Cat Add Detail screen is composed of three sections. All sections must be completed in order to submit the catalog addition request. Section 1 contains information about the manufactured item. Waste Management

This section describes how tcmIS can be used for waste management functions including on-site containerized waste transfers and waste pickups for off-site disposal.

Some client sites have elected additional services under the Chemical and Gas Management Program (CGMP) where Haas TCM employees transfer containers from the point of generation to the generator storage area. It is very important for tcmIS waste users to understand the difference between on-site waste transfers and waste pickups for off-site disposal. In tcmIS, a waste transfer refers to the transfer of containerized waste from the point of generation to the generator storage area. A waste pickup refers to the shipment of containerized waste from the generator storage area to the off-site disposal location.

For those individuals that use tcmIS for ordering chemicals, the logon for waste management is identical to that for chemical ordering. After entering a valid logon ID and password, the waste management functions of tcmIS can be accessed. These functions include: Waste Catalog, Waste Transfer Management, Waste Storage Management, and Waste Shipping.
