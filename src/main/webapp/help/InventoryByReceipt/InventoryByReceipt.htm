Inventory By Receipt

+ Larger Font | + Smaller Font

The Inventory By Receipt page provides information regarding current stock at a hub. The Inventory By Receipt page can be accessed from the Reports menu by selecting Inventory and then Inventory By Receipt. The Inventory By Receipt page is launched in a new tab.

Figure 1

Select the Operating Entity from the corresponding drop-down list. Select the Hub and Inventory Group from the corresponding drops-down lists. You may select My Hubs or My Inventory Groups to see all hubs and/or inventory groups for which you have permission.

Click Search to see all Receipt IDs currently in inventory.

Figure 2

For each Receipt ID in inventory. the quantity, cost, lot status, manufacturer lot number, warehouse bin location, expiration date, and date of receipt is displayed.

You may limit the results to only Receipt IDs that were received in a given time period by entering dates in the Date of Receipt boxes by clicking in the box and selecting a date from the calendar. Click Search.

Figure 3

You may limit the results to only material in specific warehouse bin locations by entering by locations in the Bin From and To boxes and clicking Search.

Figure 4

You may include multiple lot statuses in the results by holding the CTRL key and clicking multiple items in the Lot Status list box. Click Search.

Figure 5

You may exclude specific lot statuses from the results by selecting Exclude as opposed to Include. You may also exclude multiple lot statuses in the results by holding the CTRL key and clicking multiple items in the Lot Status list box. Click Search.

Figure 6

Click the Lot Status Legend link to see a summary and description of each lot status.

If you hover above some data cells, a tool tip will pop up and display the contents of the data cell. Typically tool tips will appear on columns where the data in the cell contains over 30 characters and the default column width does not display the entire contents of the cell. Use this feature to display the contents of cells without widening the column.

Figure 7

You may sort by any column by clicking the column header. Click the column header again to sort in descending order.

Figure 8

Click the Create Excel File button to export the results into Excel format.
